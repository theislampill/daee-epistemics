# R2 — The Reminder (Eliciting the Basic Belief)

**Register:** Reformed Epistemology / Basic Belief
**Deploy when:** The internalist criterion has been cleared or is not the barrier; directing attention to pre-argumentative recognition.

The most powerful move in this register is not an argument. It is an invitation to attention. What happens, pre-argumentatively, when the interlocutor stands before the night sky, holds a newborn, faces genuine mortality? What arises in the moment of beauty, of moral horror, of inexplicable gratitude?

For many people, what arises is not nothing. There is a movement — however faint — toward acknowledgment: something that feels less like a deduction and more like a recognition. Name this as the fiṭrah doing its natural work. The prophets do not deliver new information to passive recipients. They remind. They reawaken what is already present.

**The texture of R2 — what to do when recognition opens:**
When the interlocutor shows a sign that something has moved — a pause, a softening, an admission that "yes, there is something there" — do not press immediately for a conclusion: "So you agree that God exists?" This collapses what has opened. Recognition at the moment of opening is fragile. It has not yet been named, organized, or defended against.

What to do: be quiet, or ask a question that allows the recognition to deepen rather than one that demands it declare itself. "What is that, do you think?" rather than "Does that not suggest to you that..." The recognition, if genuine, will deepen on its own. The dāʿī's role at this moment is not to capture it but to leave space for it.

**Connection:** R2 is the prophetic mode — the reminder. It operates through V5 (directing attention to signs) as the vehicle for what R2 elicits. See also `references/procedures/P4-maieutic.md` for the extended procedure.
