# P5 — Working with the Already-Believing

**For:** Believers whose belief is shallow, untested, or held by taqlīd — inherited without
genuine examination and therefore fragile under pressure. The procedure moves belief from
inherited assumption to genuine, examined conviction.

Epistemic importance: belief held by taqlīd is not epistemically stable — it is arrived at
by chance and will not survive serious intellectual pressure. The same tradition that asks
genuine investigation of skeptics asks it of believers.

## Stages

**1. Honest assessment of foundations.**
What is their belief actually based on — evidence and experience, or habit and social
belonging? Neither is shameful, but only the former is epistemically stable. The goal is to
distinguish taqlīd from taḥqīq (genuine investigation). This requires gentleness: the person
has been faithful; the issue is not fidelity but epistemic grounding.

**2. Engaging the objections directly.**
Rather than avoiding the strongest objections to theistic belief, engage them directly and
honestly. A belief that has genuinely survived the best objections is stronger than one never
tested. The dāʿī who protects a believer from objections does them no long-term service. Use
`references/case-library/INDEX.md` DO-1 through DO-10 as the objection inventory.

**3. Broadening the evidential base.**
The grounds for belief are vastly wider than the believer may have realized: cross-cultural
convergence (tawātur fiṭrī), cosmological and fine-tuning evidence, moral experience and
orphaned intuitions, scriptural testimony and its transmission, direct experience of God in
spiritual practice — all constitute genuine epistemic support. Move the believer from inherited
assumption to multi-channel warrant. Deploy E3 (`references/tactics/E3-cumulative-case.md`).

**4. Connecting to practice.**
Genuine belief is not only an intellectual position — it is an orientation of the whole person.
The connection between epistemological depth and the quality of spiritual practice is
bidirectional and reinforcing: deeper examined conviction produces more sincere practice, and
sincere practice keeps the channels of the fiṭrah clear, producing deeper and more stable
conviction. Help the believer see this connection and inhabit it. Deploy F3
(`references/tactics/F3-practice-epistemic-access.md`).
