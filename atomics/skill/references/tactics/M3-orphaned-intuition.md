# M3 — Orphaned Intuition Probe

**Type:** Meta-tactic
**Deploy when:** Post-religious or secular moral realist position; interlocutor retains moral, aesthetic, or meaning commitments whose grounding their stated framework cannot supply.

Identify the commitments they retain from a framework they have abandoned or never held. The person who believes torture is objectively wrong, that human dignity has inherent worth, that gratitude is sometimes appropriate though they cannot say to whom, carries deliverances whose grounding their stated framework cannot supply.

**The move:** Affirm the commitment without qualification — it is genuine and correct. Then ask: "What kind of universe makes this explicable? Your framework is committed to moral realism — but what grounds it?" This is not an attack on their moral commitments. It is an invitation to follow those commitments to their proper grounding.

**The orphaned intuitions as fiṭrah deliverances:** These commitments are not residue or coincidence — they are the fiṭrah operating in the domain of moral knowledge, producing recognition that has outlasted the explicit religious framework the interlocutor has abandoned. They are signs (āyāt al-anfus — signs within the soul).

**Connection:** M3 is the entry point for NS-4 (secular moral realist); see `references/case-library/profiles/ns-4-secular-moral-realist.md`. Typically followed by E3 (cumulative case including moral track) and V5 (directing attention to signs).

## Failure Conditions

**Do not deploy when:**
- The interlocutor's framework actually accounts for their commitments. A consistent error-theorist or thoroughgoing nihilist who denies objective moral facts does not carry orphaned intuitions — they deny the intuitions' objectivity outright. M3 presupposes moral realism in practice; if the interlocutor explicitly and consistently rejects that their moral commitments track objective facts, the orphaned-intuition probe has no grip. M8 (reductio: living consistently with the denial) becomes the move instead.
- NS profile has not been established. M3 is calibrated to NS-3 and NS-4 profiles (post-religious, secular moral realist). Deploying on an interlocutor whose NS profile is unknown risks pressing commitments they do not actually hold and attributing orphaned intuitions that are absent.
- The commitments are performative rather than substantive. Some interlocutors invoke moral language for social signaling without treating their claims as tracking objective facts. Pressing for grounding when the commitment is performative generates confusion rather than movement. Confirm through earlier conversation that the interlocutor treats their moral claims as genuinely binding.
- Hawā or gharaḍ is the primary operative deformation. If the interlocutor's resistance to theism is volitional rather than epistemically motivated, affirming their moral commitments and inviting grounding will not generate movement — the grounding-question will be deflected, deferred, or absorbed without traction. F2 must surface the volitional dimension first.

**M3 fails when:**
- The interlocutor accepts the observation that their framework cannot ground the commitment, but treats this as a problem for their framework to solve later, not as a pointer to anything beyond it. This is an internal-reform response; the conversation has moved from M3's territory to a philosophical research project. Note the opening, allow it to remain, and do not press immediately.
- The interlocutor provides a naturalist grounding (evolutionary ethics, social contract, Humean sentiment). This is not a deflection — it is a substantive philosophical claim. Do not loop M3; engage the specific grounding claim on its merits. Route to E3 (whether the naturalist grounding fully accounts for the full texture of the commitment, including its phenomenological character as binding and not merely emergent).

## IR-Visible Consequences

When M3 is live:
- **routing_gate**: marks orphaned-intuition upstream finding active; NS-4 route opened as a permissible continuation
- **matched_modules**: expands to include E3 (cumulative case, moral track) and V5 (directing attention to signs as external counterpart to M3's inward probe) once commitment-grounding question is in play
- **held_material**: fuller E3/V5 content is held pending M3's probe landing — do not deploy the full cumulative case before the orphaned intuition has been named and affirmed
- **output_shape**: bounded-single-pass — M3 makes one probe move; reassessment governs continuation

If M3 lands (grounding gap acknowledged):
- commitment-grounding-gap active in case-state; E3 and V5 released as available next moves

If M3 does not land (interlocutor denies moral realism or accepts the gap without movement):
- If nihilism/error theory: route to M8 (reductio of consistent nihilism practice)
- If internal-reform response: note opening; allow time; do not iterate M3

## Minimal-Pair Discriminators

**M3 vs. M8 (reductio):**
M3 is affirmative — it affirms the interlocutor's moral commitments and asks what makes them coherent. M8 is adversarial — it follows the interlocutor's stated framework to a conclusion they cannot accept. M3 when the interlocutor holds moral realism in practice but cannot ground it; M8 when the interlocutor explicitly denies moral realism and needs to see the cost of consistency.

**M3 vs. R2 (the reminder):**
Both move inward — M3 probes commitments whose grounding is absent; R2 elicits pre-argumentative recognition. M3 operates in the intellectual domain (what grounds your commitments?); R2 operates in the phenomenological domain (what arises involuntarily when you are present to certain experiences?). M3 when the entry point is moral or meaning commitments; R2 when the entry point is experiential or aesthetic recognition.

**M3 vs. V5 (directing attention to signs):**
V5 directs attention to external signs — creation, natural order, human experience across cultures. M3 probes internal signs — the orphaned moral and aesthetic intuitions within the interlocutor's own noetic structure. M3 precedes V5 when the interlocutor's own commitments are the entry point; V5 follows M3 when external counterparts to those internal deliverances become relevant.

## Hold / Release Discipline

M3 issues a single bounded probe — affirm the commitment, ask the grounding question, stop. Do not deploy E3 or V5 until the grounding gap has been acknowledged or the interlocutor has responded to the probe.

**Release signal:** The interlocutor has acknowledged that their stated framework cannot fully account for the commitment's objectivity, even partially. The acknowledgment need not be explicit — a pause, a "I've thought about that" without a ready answer, or a shift in how they describe the commitment (from "I think torture is wrong" to "torture just is wrong") signals the probe has landed.

**Do not loop M3:** If the interlocutor provides a naturalist grounding or denies the commitment's objectivity, M3 has done its diagnostic work. The case-state update determines the next move. Re-stating the probe escalates without advancing.

## Anti-Pattern Guard

**False attribution of orphaned intuitions:** Attributing orphaned intuitions to an interlocutor who is a consistent framework-denier (nihilist, error-theorist). Confirming that the interlocutor actually holds moral realism in practice is prerequisite. Pressing the grounding question when the interlocutor genuinely does not hold their moral commitments as tracking objective facts generates confusion and reads as not listening.

**Inversion into accusation:** Framing the probe as "your framework is inconsistent" rather than "your commitments are real — what makes them real?" The probe is affirmative, not critical. The orphaned-intuition observation functions as an invitation, not an indictment.
