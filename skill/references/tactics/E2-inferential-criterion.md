# E2 — Pressing the Inferential Criterion

**Register:** Evidentialist
**Deploy when:** The interlocutor holds that inferential argument is *necessary* for warranted theistic belief.

Interrogate the criterion itself: by what argument does one know that argument is required? This generates a regress.

**The key move:** The interlocutor's belief in other minds, the external world, the past, the reliability of memory — none of which rest on argument — shows that basic belief is already operating in their noetic structure. The criterion that disqualifies theistic belief equally disqualifies these. It proves too much.

The interlocutor must either:
- Relax the criterion (toward Soft Evidentialism — evidence may be required but is not the sole gateway)
- Acknowledge that basic belief is legitimate (toward Reformed Epistemology — theistic basic belief is back in contention)

**Connection:** Once the criterion is loosened, move to R1 (challenging the internalist criterion) for the Reformed register, or to E1 + E3 for the evidentialist register.

## Failure Conditions

**Do not deploy when:**
- The case already operates with basic belief. If the interlocutor already accepts that some beliefs are warranted non-inferentially in other domains and has not explicitly demanded argument for theism, E2 presses a criterion that has not been asserted. Go directly to R1 (challenge the specifically theistic restriction on basic belief) or R3 (proper-function account of the interlocutor's own recognition).
- The deformation is hawā or gharaḍ. The inferential-criterion demand is serving as cover for volitional resistance. F2 must surface the volitional dimension first; clearing the criterion without addressing its function as cover does nothing.
- The NS profile is NS-7 (theistic evidentialist). The interlocutor already accepts theism; E2 is irrelevant.
- The framework has already been cleared by doubt-vs-skepticism. Repeating E2's reductio after doubt-vs-skepticism has established burden-inversion is redundant.

**E2 fails when:**
- The interlocutor accepts the reductio but carves out theism as the exception: "basic belief is fine for other minds, but theistic belief is a higher-stakes claim." This shifts the case from the universal criterion to a specifically theistic restriction — now R1 territory.
- The interlocutor doubles down on global internalism (denying basic warrant for other minds, memory, the past too). The analogy is absorbed. M1 (self-refutation: a global internalist cannot warrant the internalist position by its own standard) is required before E2's analogy can gain purchase.

## IR-Visible Consequences

When E2 is live:
- **routing_gate**: marks criterion-challenge active; opens the basic-belief route (R1 → R2 → R3 chain) as a permissible next move
- **matched_modules**: expands to include R1 when the criterion is challenged on Reformed grounds; E1 + E3 when the interlocutor shifts toward Soft Evidentialism
- **held_material**: E2 holds nothing itself; it is a single criterion-clearing move
- **output_shape**: bounded-single-pass — E2 makes its move; reassessment governs continuation

If E2 lands (criterion loosened):
- Case-state notes criterion-loosened; restoration-target shifts to basic-belief deployment or evidential content

If E2 does not land (criterion defended):
- Re-examine deformation axis: is the criterion a genuine epistemic commitment (shubhah — M1 required) or a proxy for hawā/gharaḍ (F2 governs)?

## Minimal-Pair Discriminators

**E2 vs. doubt-vs-skepticism:**
doubt-vs-skepticism challenges whether evidence is the default epistemic requirement at all, inverting the burden of proof. E2 accepts that the interlocutor holds an evidential criterion and shows it proves too much. Sequence: doubt-vs-skepticism first when the evidence demand is posed as the neutral default; E2 when the interlocutor is committed to the criterion and needs to see its self-defeating character.

**E2 vs. R1:**
E2 presses at the meta-level (argument is not required for warrant in any domain); R1 presses at the object-level (theistic basic belief specifically is not disqualified). E2 first when the criterion is stated universally; R1 when the specifically theistic restriction remains after E2 has softened universal internalism.

**E2 vs. M1 (self-refutation):**
M1 shows an argument's premises undermine its conclusion. E2 shows the evidential criterion, applied consistently, disqualifies foundational beliefs the interlocutor already holds. Both produce a reductio, but M1 targets a specific argument's logical structure; E2 targets the practical self-refutation of the criterion across all basic belief. Run M1 first if there is a structurally self-refuting argument; E2 when the criterion itself is the problem.

## Hold / Release Discipline

E2 does not hold downstream content. It clears a single upstream barrier — the inferential-criterion requirement.

**What E2 enables:** R1 (Reformed route: challenge the specifically theistic restriction on basic belief) and E1 + E3 (Evidentialist route: once basic belief is in contention, broaden the evidential scope). Both are held pending E2's criterion challenge.

**Release signal:** The interlocutor has acknowledged that the analogy creates a problem for the criterion — even without fully conceding. Shifting to defend a theism-specific carve-out also signals E2 has done its work; R1 now takes over.

**Do not loop E2:** If the criterion is not shifting, do not repeat it with more force. Update the case-state — the criterion may be serving a volitional function (F2) or may require M1 first before the analogy can land.

## Anti-Pattern Guard

**Over-selection:** Deploying E2 when the interlocutor has not explicitly demanded inferential argument. Pressing a criterion that was not asserted reads as a non-sequitur and introduces a criterion-debate the interlocutor was not running. Confirm the criterion is actually operative before deploying.

**Under-sequencing:** Running E2 without reading the deformation axis first. If the criterion demand is genuine intellectual commitment (shubhah), E2 is correct. If it is rhetorical cover for hawā or gharaḍ, E2 clears nothing. Symptom: E2 lands but no movement follows, because the criterion was not the real barrier.
