# The Inductive Method for Identifying Fiṭrī Deliverables

**Deploy when:** Grounding E4 or any tawātur fiṭrī claim; determining what is genuinely fiṭrī vs. culturally variable; responding to diversity objections that conflate foundation with superstructure.

## The Method

The method for identifying what is genuinely fiṭrī is inductive: survey what is universally held among those whose cognition is unimpaired by the relevant deformation; treat that universality as evidence of what sound fiṭrah actually delivers.

This is not an appeal to numbers (the genetic fallacy). It is the application of the tawātur principle to cognitive deliverances: when a vast number of human beings across all times and cultures, without possibility of collusion, report the same innate cognitive intuition, this constitutes pan-human tawātur on the level of fiṭrah deliverances (tawātur fiṭrī).

## The Foundation/Superstructure Distinction

The inductive survey reveals two layers:

**Foundation (fiṭrī-invariant across cultures and times):**
- That being as such requires a source
- That moral obligation is real and categorical
- That the purely material is inadequate to account for human existence
- That there is something it is like to be a human being that points beyond the physical

This does not vary significantly across cultures when the seven deformations are controlled for.

**Superstructure (culturally variable):**
- Which specific events are attributed to which agents
- Ritual practices and their specific forms
- Cosmological mythologies and narratives
- Theological elaborations and doctrinal systems

This varies — and its variation may be explicable through cognitive biases, cultural transmission, and the seven deformations. The tawātur fiṭrī argument concerns the foundation, not the superstructure.

## Application

When interlocutor cites religious diversity to defeat E4: press the distinction. "I am not claiming that all religious traditions agree in their superstructure. I am claiming that there is a cross-cultural invariant foundation — the recognition that being requires a source, that moral obligation is real, that the purely material is insufficient. Explain *that* foundation. The diversity you are pointing to is in the superstructure."

When establishing what is genuinely fiṭrī: apply the inductive survey. Is this recognition present across cultures when deformations are controlled for? Is it involuntary? Is it stable under genuine attentiveness? If yes, the tawātur fiṭrī thesis applies.

## Self-Application: When DO-7 Is Directed at the Fiṭrah Account Itself

The cognitive science of religion (DO-7 — HADD, evolutionary debunking, natural religion
hypotheses) is most often deployed by interlocutors against *theistic belief in general*. But
a philosophically sophisticated interlocutor may direct it specifically at the tawātur fiṭrī
thesis — arguing that the claimed universality of theistic recognition is itself a cognitive
artifact, and that the foundation/superstructure distinction is ad hoc: whatever cognitive
science explains gets reclassified as "superstructure," and whatever it cannot yet explain gets
called "foundation."

The answer is the same foundation/superstructure distinction applied reflexively:

**What cognitive science of religion actually explains:**
HADD and related mechanisms account for the tendency to attribute agency to specific natural
events — lightning, flood, illness, wind. They explain why animism, polytheism, and the
population of the world with spirits and named agents is a cross-cultural phenomenon. They
account for the superstructural elaborations.

**What cognitive science of religion does not explain:**
- The recognition that *being as such* requires a source — this is not an agent-attribution to
  a natural event but a metaphysical intuition about existence itself
- The recognition that moral obligation is *categorically* binding, not merely socially
  enforced — HADD does not produce moral realism
- The recognition that the material order is *insufficient* to account for consciousness,
  value, and meaning — this is not over-attribution of agency but under-attribution of
  sufficiency to the physical
- The involuntary character of gratitude that exceeds any finite recipient — this is not an
  agent-detection response to an environmental stimulus

These foundational recognitions are precisely what HADD and social-cognition hypotheses do
not explain. When the interlocutor claims that evolutionary cognitive science explains
*theistic belief*, they mean the superstructural elaborations — the specific gods, the rituals,
the narratives. The foundation is not explained away by explaining the superstructure built on it.

**The self-consistency point:** The tawātur fiṭrī thesis claims universality for the foundation,
not the superstructure. The inductive survey applies at the level of: does every culture, across
time, with deformations controlled for, recognize that existence requires a source, that moral
obligation is real, and that the material is insufficient? It does. HADD explains the
superstructural variation; it does not touch this foundational convergence. The ad hoc charge
fails: the distinction between foundation and superstructure is specified independently of
cognitive science's reach — the specification is prior, not reactive.
