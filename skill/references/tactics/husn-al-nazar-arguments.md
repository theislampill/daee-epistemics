# Ḥusn al-Naẓar — Inferential Arguments for the Impaired Fiṭrah

Load when ḥusn al-naẓar has been selected (see `references/sound-reason-epistemology.md` §5 for
deployment conditions and sequencing). These are not primary means to theistic belief —
they are instruments for clearing the path back to fiṭrah-operation when the fiṭrah is
too impaired to respond directly to signs (āyāt).

Always sequence correctly: V2 (clear the framework) → M1 (self-refutation check) →
deploy the argument calibrated to this interlocutor → return to V5/R2 (signs and the
reminder) as soon as the intellectual obstacle is cleared.

Select the argument most calibrated to the interlocutor's existing commitments and
sensibilities. No single argument need be decisive; E3 (cumulative case) combines them.

## Cosmological — Temporal Origination

Every temporally originated thing requires a cause. The universe is temporally
originated — established by the impossibility of an actual infinite regress of prior
causes. Therefore the universe requires a cause that is itself not temporally originated:
uncaused, necessary in itself.

**Deploy when:** The interlocutor accepts causal reasoning and the impossibility of
actual infinite regress. **Target:** The naturalist's assumption of causal closure —
that the physical causal order is complete and self-sufficient.

**Al-Ghazali's planetary regress argument:** Imagine the solar system has existed for
eternity. Saturn revolves around the sun once for every thirty Earth years. This means
Earth has completed more revolutions than Saturn — yet an eternal universe must entail
both planets have made an equally infinite number of revolutions. The number cannot be
both equal and unequal simultaneously. "Each number has no end, yet some of them are
lesser than others. This is clearly impossible." An actual infinite regress cannot exist
in the real world because it would have to be both complete and incomplete at once.

**Leibniz's explanatory gap:** An infinite regression of causes does not provide
sufficient reason for why there is any world at all or why it is as it is. "However far
back we might go into previous states, we will never find in those states a complete
explanation for why, indeed, there is any world at all." The regress hypothesis does not
explain — it defers. The question "why is there something rather than nothing" is not
answered by pointing to a prior something.

**Imām Abū Ḥanīfa's ship argument:** When confronted by philosophers skeptical of the
Creator, Abū Ḥanīfa proposed: imagine a ship on the Tigris, moving by itself, loading
and unloading its cargo, navigating and anchoring without anyone to manage it. His
interlocutors responded: "This could never happen." Abū Ḥanīfa replied: "If it is
impossible for a ship, then how is it possible for the entire world in all of its
vastness?" The intuition is not generated by abstract premises — it is activated by
attention to what the person already knows about order and agency.

**The "who created the Creator" objection:** This commits a category mistake. The
cosmological argument already establishes that the necessary being must logically
exist — it is by definition uncaused. Asking who created the Creator is like asking
what blue tastes like: the question misapplies a category. More precisely: if the
Creator had a creator, He would be created Himself and would not be the Creator of
everything. The impossibility is internal to the concept of a necessary being.

## Teleological — Design and the Islamic Scholarly Heritage

The physical constants, initial conditions, and structural order of the universe are
calibrated within extraordinarily narrow tolerances for the existence of complex life.
The hypothesis that this calibration is the product of intelligence is more parsimonious
than brute fact or an unobserved multiverse.

**Deploy when:** The interlocutor is scientifically literate. **Frame as:** Inference
to the best explanation, not argument from ignorance.

**The method of the Four Imāms:** When asked about the existence of the Creator, the
Imāms of Islamic jurisprudence each directed questioners to specific signs in creation
rather than to abstract philosophical premises. Argument by design is most effective
when it activates the fiṭrah through directed attention to a specific phenomenon:

- **Imām Abū Ḥanīfah** asked an atheist to consider how the world could move, load,
  and navigate on its own without a manager (the ship argument above).
- **Imām Mālik** told the Caliph to reflect upon the diversity of languages, voices,
  and sounds in creation — each distinct, all coherent within one reality.
- **Imām al-Shāfiʿī** asked a questioner to consider the single species of leaf eaten
  by different creatures that produces silk (silkworms), honey (bees), milk (animals),
  and musk — one input, radically different but purposively organized outputs.
- **Imām Aḥmad** asked a person to ponder new life emerging from an ordinary egg.

The wisdom: the design argument is accessible to anyone regardless of training, and
when presented as directed attention rather than syllogism, it activates the fiṭrah
rather than the disputative intellect. The sign indicates; it does not argue. See V5.

**Ibn al-Qayyim's watermill:** Imagine a watermill revolving over a river in perfection
— its tools complete, its parts measured with excellence, presiding over a grand garden,
watering every fruit and crop as needed, distributing its produce without disorder.
Would any observer conclude this has no creator, harvester, or manager? The intuition
is immediate. Extended to the universe: "What do you find that your mind tells you?"

**Against "nature as designer":** If nature is conceded to exhibit will, knowledge,
power, and wisdom, then it is simply being called by a different name. "If it surely
sustains itself and has complete knowledge, power, will, and wisdom, then this is the
Creator, the Designer — so why do you refer to Him as nature?" If nature has none of
these qualities, it cannot account for the order observed.

**Against the infinite monkey theorem:** Order produced by pure random chance over
infinite time relies on an impossible actual infinite. The mathematical probability of
random processes producing even minor complex order is so small it functions as zero —
the odds of chance producing fourteen lines of Shakespeare are calculated at 10 to the
690th power, against a universe of roughly 10 to the 80th atomic particles.

## Moral — Objective Value Requires Grounding

Moral facts are objective, mind-independent, categorically binding, and reliably tracked
by properly functioning human cognition. These features are most coherently explained
by grounding in a mind that is the source of value. The secular alternatives —
non-natural moral properties, quasi-realism, Kantian rational legislation — carry heavy
metaphysical costs and remain philosophically contested.

**Deploy when:** The interlocutor is a secular moral realist, or holds moral commitments
whose grounding their stated framework cannot supply. **Primary vehicle:** M3 (orphaned
intuition probe) — affirm their commitment to moral realism, then ask what kind of
universe makes it explicable.

## Consciousness — Normative Character (Deploy Track B)

**Track A (existence only):** Consciousness exists and is not physically explicable →
inference to non-physical reality. Vulnerable: panpsychism and mysterianism are
alternative gap-fillers.

**Track B — normative character (deploy this one):** Human consciousness has a specific
normative character — irreducible first-person ipseity (āyāt al-anfus), truth-tracking
rather than mere fitness-tracking, categorical moral responsiveness, attentiveness to
beauty as pointing beyond itself, gratitude capable of exceeding any finite recipient —
that is explicable if and only if consciousness is oriented toward and constituted for
relationship with a personal source.

Panpsychism accounts for proto-experiential properties; it does not account for the
specifically normative, oriented, truth-tracking character of human consciousness.
Mysterianism names the gap without filling it. Track B under-determines its conclusion
far less than Track A and is substantially more resistant to naturalistic alternatives.

**Deploy when:** The interlocutor is philosophically sophisticated and has engaged with
philosophy of mind. **Press:** Not "why is there something it is like to be you?" but
"why does that something orient itself toward truth, register moral obligation
categorically, find beauty pointing beyond itself, experience gratitude that exceeds
any finite recipient?"
