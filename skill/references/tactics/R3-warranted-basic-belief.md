# R3 — Warranted Basic Belief and Proper Function

**Register:** Reformed Epistemology / Basic Belief
**Deploy when:** Applying the proper-function framework directly to the interlocutor's own involuntary experience of theistic-type recognition.

Ask the interlocutor to account for moments where theistic-type awareness arose involuntarily and unbidden — and ask what produced it. Not "why do you believe?" but "what was that? Where did it come from? What does it mean that it arose without your choosing it?"

**The epistemic significance of involuntariness:** Beliefs produced by involuntarily functioning cognitive faculties in appropriate conditions are precisely what the basic-belief framework holds to be properly basic — warranted without further argument. If the interlocutor cannot account for the involuntary recognition within their own framework, that is an internal pressure point, not an external imposition.

**The proper-function account:** The fiṭrah is a real cognitive faculty. Its proper function is the production of warranted basic theistic belief when encountering āyāt. When it produces such recognition involuntarily, without the interlocutor's deliberate choice, that is evidence of a faculty functioning properly — not a psychological curiosity to be explained away. See `references/sound-reason-epistemology.md` §2–3 for the full account.

**Connection:** R3 follows R2 — R2 elicits the recognition, R3 engages the interlocutor's philosophical account of what it is.

## Failure Conditions

**Do not deploy when:**
- R2 has not surfaced recognition. R3 asks the interlocutor to examine an involuntary experience of theistic-type recognition — but if no such recognition has been elicited or acknowledged, there is nothing for R3 to engage. Deploying R3 without R2 having first surfaced something asks the interlocutor to examine an experience they have not acknowledged having. The move reads as an assertion that they must have had such experiences.
- The interlocutor genuinely denies any experience of theistic-type recognition. If R2 has been deployed and the interlocutor sincerely reports that nothing of this kind arises — not dismissive denial but sincere reportage that such experiences are absent — R3 cannot operate on absent material. F3 (creating conditions for recognition) governs next; R3 becomes available after F3 has prepared the noetic ground.
- The internalist criterion has not been at least challenged. If the criterion that theistic belief requires argument is still fully operative, R3's proper-function analysis will be dismissed: "your faculty producing the recognition doesn't make it warranted — I need argument, not faculty output." R1 must have at least challenged the criterion before R3 applies the proper-function account; otherwise the criterion is used to dismiss the account as circular.
- The DO-7 (debunking argument) is raised but has not been addressed. DO-7 argues that theistic recognition is fully explained by evolutionary psychology or neuroscience, making the faculty explanation redundant. If this debunking argument is live and unaddressed, R3's proper-function account is absorbed by the debunking response: "you call it proper function; I call it evolved sociality." DO-7 + M1 must address the debunking argument before R3's proper-function account can carry weight.

**R3 fails when:**
- The interlocutor accepts that they had involuntary recognition but attributes it to psychology (projection, pattern-matching, evolved sociality) without engaging whether this attribution exhausts the explanation. This is DO-7 territory. Update case-state to include DO-7 as live; do not iterate R3 without first engaging the debunking argument.
- The interlocutor accepts the proper-function framework in principle but denies that the fiṭrah is functioning properly in their case ("maybe I'm broken in that way"). This is an unusual but sincere response. Route to R2's deeper form (F3) and a pastoral register; the proper-function question has now become personal rather than philosophical.

## Minimal-Pair Discriminators

**R3 vs. R2 (the reminder):**
R2 is phenomenological — it elicits the recognition, creates space for it, and holds the space without pressing. R3 is analytical — it takes the recognition that R2 has elicited and asks what it means philosophically (what produced it, what the involuntariness implies for warrant). R2 precedes R3; they are not alternatives but a sequence. Do not run R3 in place of R2 as a way of eliciting recognition — R3 presupposes the recognition is already acknowledged.

**R3 vs. R1 (internalist criterion):**
R1 challenges the criterion that bars the door to theistic basic belief (object-level: why specifically theism?). R3 applies the proper-function framework to the interlocutor's own experience (inward: what is this recognition, and what produced it?). R1 operates outside the interlocutor's own experience, on the philosophical criterion; R3 operates inside it, on what the interlocutor has themselves acknowledged arising. Both are Reformed-register moves; R1 clears the external barrier, R3 presses the internal pressure point.

**R3 vs. E2 (inferential criterion):**
E2 presses the meta-level reductio: the universal inferential criterion proves too much. R3 applies proper-function directly to lived experience. E2 is outward and philosophical; R3 is inward and phenomenological. E2 when the case is at the level of general epistemological criterion; R3 when the case has moved to the interlocutor's own involuntary experience.

## Hold / Release Discipline

P7 Stop-2 governs R3. R3 makes one analytical move — asks what produced the involuntary recognition, what involuntariness implies for warrant — then stops. The interlocutor's response determines all continuation; no content is pre-queued.

**Prerequisite chain:** R2 must have elicited recognition before R3 can operate. R1 must have at least challenged the criterion before R3's proper-function account can carry weight without being dismissed by the criterion. The sequencing is not optional.

**Release from R3:** R3 releases to the interlocutor's response. If they engage the proper-function account, E3 becomes available for the fuller cumulative picture. If DO-7 is raised, R3 pauses pending DO-7 + M1 treatment. If the recognition is now acknowledged as philosophically significant, the case is in a new state — re-read before continuing.

## Anti-Pattern Guard

**Deploying without R2 having surfaced recognition:** R3 asks the interlocutor to examine an involuntary experience. If R2 has not first elicited and acknowledged that experience, R3 is asserting that such an experience must exist. This reads as presumptuous and generates defensiveness rather than philosophical engagement.

**Using R3 as R2 replacement:** R3 is not a way to combine elicitation and philosophical analysis in a single move. The elicitation (R2) and the analysis (R3) serve different functions; collapsing them means the analysis begins before the recognition has been confirmed as present, generating a philosophical account of something the interlocutor has not acknowledged.
