# M1 — Self-Refutation Check

**Type:** Meta-tactic
**Deploy when:** Before constructing any response — examine whether the objection's premises undermine the objection itself.

When present, deliver this move cleanly and early. Do not bury it. The interlocutor who grasps it grasps it immediately; elaboration allows them to find somewhere to hide.

**Examples:**
- The evolutionary debunking argument uses the cognitive faculties it declares unreliable
- The divine transcendence objection cannot be stated without using creaturely language to make claims about what exceeds creaturely language
- The proportionality norm (Clifford's Principle) is not itself held in proportion to evidence
- The claim "only empirically testable propositions are meaningful" is not itself empirically testable

**Delivery:** State the objection at its strongest. Then: "But notice that the premises of this argument, applied consistently, also undermine the argument itself." Show the move cleanly. Stop. Do not pile on.

**Distinguishing from M1-P:** M1 is about premises undermining conclusions through logical entailment. M1-P (performative self-refutation) is about the act of asserting the position enacting what it denies. They are different — and often both apply simultaneously.

## The Phenomenological Falsification of Naturalism

A stronger move than the standard self-application check. Distinct in form: instead of showing
naturalism cannot ground itself, this shows naturalism is directly falsified by a phenomenon it
predicts should not occur.

**The argument:**
1. If naturalism is true, all meaningful explanation operates within the physical causal order —
   no experience should allow coherent conception of explanatory frameworks beyond it.
2. But human experience *does* allow coherent conception of explanations beyond the natural
   order — including God. This is not merely claimed by theists: avowed atheists acknowledge the
   persistent, involuntary, apparently irresistible character of this conceptual capacity.
   Dawkins: biology gives "the appearance of having been designed for a purpose." Shermer: "we
   are natural-born supernaturalists... the gods will always be with us because they are
   hard-wired into our brains." The phenomenon is conceded; only its interpretation is contested.
3. Therefore naturalism is false — not merely unable to ground itself, but directly contradicted
   by the phenomenology it would need to explain away.

**The distinction from M1's standard move:** Standard M1 shows the position is self-undermining
(its own standard defeats it). This argument shows the position is *empirically falsified* —
the phenomenon it predicts should be impossible (coherent, irresistible theistic conception)
actually occurs. A naturalist cannot dismiss theistic cognition as error without invoking the
very cognitive faculties they have declared unreliable to do the dismissing.

**Delivery:** Do not use this as a rhetorical flourish. Use it against an interlocutor who
has committed to naturalism as an exhaustive ontology — not just as a methodological stance.
State the prediction, then show the phenomenon. The concessions of Dawkins and Shermer are
especially useful: the opponent's own authorities acknowledge the datum.

**Connection:** This argument grounds V2 (the contamination of reason) and feeds E1 (broadening
the evidential scope). The capacity to conceive God coherently — prior to argument, involuntary,
cross-cultural — is itself an āyah.
