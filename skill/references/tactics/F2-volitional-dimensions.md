# F2 — Foregrounding the Volitional Dimensions

**Register:** Fideist
**Deploy when:** Interlocutor appears intellectually moved but remains volitionally resistant; or deformation triage (M5) identifies gharaḍ or hawā as primary.

When an interlocutor appears intellectually moved but remains volitionally resistant, additional argument is the wrong instrument. Shift registers: what is actually at stake for this person? Is the barrier genuinely a shubhah, or does it have to do with what acknowledgment would cost them?

**The critical warning:** Presenting intellectual content to someone whose barrier is vested interest or entrenched bias does not merely fail — it provides more material for resistance to organize around, and actively hardens the barrier.

**The move:** Careful, empathic attention to the real shape of the resistance. What presents as intellectual objection is often, at its root, a volitional or relational problem: gharaḍ or hawā, not iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha. Foregrounding the volitional dimension is not accusation — it is genuine pastoral attention. Ask; do not assume.

"Is this an intellectual objection, or is there something personally at stake in the question?"

**Connection:** See `references/diagnostics/seven-deformations.md` §6 (gharaḍ) and §2 (hawā) for full treatment. F3 follows if F2 opens genuine inquiry.
