# M7 - The Definition Anchor

**Type:** Meta-tactic
**Deploy when:** The interlocutor challenges a word rather than engaging the underlying claim; a terminological dispute is being used to avoid substantive engagement.

Common forms: "religion is too wide a concept," "that is not what guidance means," "you are using 'reason' differently than I do."

This is either genuine need for clarification or a deflection tactic. Either way the procedure is the same.

## Four-step procedure

1. Acknowledge that terminology can be contested.
2. Offer a precise working definition for the current discussion.
3. Invite agreement or counter-proposal.
4. Proceed with the agreed or stipulated definition, noting explicitly that the argument still stands under it.

The key move is to show that the argument's force does not depend on the particular word chosen.

## Scope boundary

M7 is not the owner of broader conception-capture problems. When contradiction pressure is being generated by silent redefinition, public-language capture, universals/particulars confusion, or mental vs. extra-mental confusion, route to `references/diagnostics/definition-discipline.md`. M7 handles the local lexical dispute; the diagnostic file handles the wider definition-governance burden.
