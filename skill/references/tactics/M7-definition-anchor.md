# M7 — The Definition Anchor

**Type:** Meta-tactic
**Deploy when:** Interlocutor challenges a word rather than engaging the underlying claim; terminological dispute is being used (consciously or not) to avoid substantive engagement.

Common forms: "religion is too wide a concept," "that's not what guidance means," "you're using 'reason' differently than I do."

This is either genuine need for clarification or a deflection tactic. Either way the procedure is the same.

**Four-step procedure:**
1. Acknowledge: terminology is genuinely contestable and clarity is necessary.
2. Offer a precise, functional definition — not a dictionary definition but a working description of what the term picks out in the context of the current discussion.
3. Invite agreement or counter-proposal: if the interlocutor offers a different definition, examine whether the argument holds under that definition as well.
4. Proceed with the agreed or stipulated definition, noting explicitly that the argument holds under it.

**The key move:** Show that the argument's force does not depend on the particular word chosen. The underlying claim — that every person operates with a comprehensive framework of bindings, that some guidance is either available or not, that reason either tracks truth or it does not — holds regardless of what we call it. The word is not what is doing the work.

**Diagnostic use:** Terminological challenge is often ẓann or juhūd in disguise — avoiding the substance by disputing the frame. The definition anchor closes that exit without confrontation.
