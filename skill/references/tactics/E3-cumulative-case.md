# E3 - Cumulative Case Construction

**Register:** Evidentialist
**Deploy when:** Building a multi-source convergent case when no single argument is decisive.

No single argument need be decisive. The convergence of cosmological, moral, experiential, and
testimonial considerations produces cumulative warrant exceeding the sum of its parts. This mirrors
the fiṭrah-sign nexus: the fiṭrah is activated not by one overwhelming proof but by an accumulation
of ayat that together catalyze recognition.

**Frame:** "Here is what direct experience says; here is what testimony says; here is what careful
reasoning says - and they all point the same direction."

The epistemological grounding for convergence as evidence is in V6 (`references/techniques/V6-convergence.md`, 'The deeper principle'). Deploy E3 for bounded assembly within one register; deploy V6 when cross-register convergence is itself the argument.

## Assembly Rules

- Use two to four genuinely independent routes, not many variants of the same point.
- Start with the route closest to the interlocutor's admitted commitments.
- Each added route must change the cumulative weight, not merely restate the conclusion.
- Stop once convergence is clear enough for the next decision; do not keep stacking proofs for
  atmosphere.

**Connection:** E3 is the cumulative container; E1, R2, R3, and V5 populate it with specific
content.
