# R2 — The Reminder (Eliciting the Basic Belief)

**Register:** Reformed Epistemology / Basic Belief
**Deploy when:** The internalist criterion has been cleared or is not the barrier; directing attention to pre-argumentative recognition.

The most powerful move in this register is not an argument. It is an invitation to attention. What happens, pre-argumentatively, when the interlocutor stands before the night sky, holds a newborn, faces genuine mortality? What arises in the moment of beauty, of moral horror, of inexplicable gratitude?

For many people, what arises is not nothing. There is a movement — however faint — toward acknowledgment: something that feels less like a deduction and more like a recognition. Name this as the fiṭrah doing its natural work. The prophets do not deliver new information to passive recipients. They remind. They reawaken what is already present.

**The texture of R2 — what to do when recognition opens:**
When the interlocutor shows a sign that something has moved — a pause, a softening, an admission that "yes, there is something there" — do not press immediately for a conclusion: "So you agree that God exists?" This collapses what has opened. Recognition at the moment of opening is fragile. It has not yet been named, organized, or defended against.

What to do: be quiet, or ask a question that allows the recognition to deepen rather than one that demands it declare itself. "What is that, do you think?" rather than "Does that not suggest to you that..." The recognition, if genuine, will deepen on its own. The dāʿī's role at this moment is not to capture it but to leave space for it.

**Connection:** R2 is the prophetic mode — the reminder. It operates through V5 (directing attention to signs) as the vehicle for what R2 elicits. The same move can be sustained as a longer maieutic procedure when the case needs extended questioning.

## Failure Conditions

**Do not deploy when:**
- The evidential or internalist framework has not been cleared. R2 requires that recognition, when elicited, can register as recognition — not be immediately filtered by an unexamined criterion that classifies it as psychological noise. If the interlocutor is still operating under an active "argument is required" criterion, the movement R2 elicits will be dismissed as anecdotal feeling, not warrant. R1 or E2 must challenge the criterion first.
- Hawā or gharaḍ is the primary deformation. R2 makes an affective opening. When volitional resistance is operative, this opening will be exploited: the interlocutor will acknowledge something moves in them and use that acknowledgment to perform sincerity while remaining unmoved. F2 must surface the volitional dimension before R2's opening can land authentically.
- Juḥūd or istikbār is the concealment mode. Recognition in these concealment modes may be present but will not be admitted. R2 eliciting something that the interlocutor will not acknowledge generates a performance of non-recognition. The diagnostic sign: the interlocutor becomes dismissive or contemptuous at the moment R2 opens space. Relational and pastoral register governs in these cases.
- Recognition is not near the surface (NS profile does not indicate any residual theistic leaning). R2 is a reminder — it works when something is present to be reminded of. If the interlocutor's noetic structure shows no recognitional material near the surface (thoroughly naturalized, no residual affective or aesthetic theistic deliverances), F3 must first create the conditions in which recognition can arise. R2 applied to a fully foreclosed noetic structure generates nothing.

**R2 fails when:**
- The interlocutor acknowledges something moves in them but refuses to characterize it as recognition of anything: "I feel something but I don't know that it points to God." This is not failure — it is partial landing. Do not press; allow the question ("What is that, do you think?") to remain open. R3 becomes available when the interlocutor is ready to examine the involuntariness philosophically.
- The interlocutor treats the elicited recognition as an emotional state with no epistemic significance. This is the criterion reasserting itself at the moment of recognition. R1 or E2 may need to return; the recognition can be held while the criterion is re-addressed.

## IR-Visible Consequences

When R2 is live:
- **routing_gate**: recognition-elicitation active; argumentative content held while the invitation space is open — do not resume argument until recognition has been acknowledged or ruled out
- **matched_modules**: R3 (proper-function analysis) becomes available once recognition has been elicited; V5 (directing attention to signs) is the vehicle through which R2 operates
- **held_material**: all argumentative content — syllogisms, evidential cases, cumulative-case structure — is held during R2's operation. The invitation is primary; argument is the wrong register for this moment
- **output_shape**: bounded-single-pass — R2 makes one opening move; the interlocutor's response governs all continuation
- **recognition_strength**: IR field updated when recognition is elicited: weak_present / acknowledged / denied

If R2 lands (recognition acknowledged, even partially):
- recognition_strength updated; held argumentative content remains held; R3 available as next move when interlocutor is ready to examine the recognition philosophically

If R2 does not land (recognition denied or no response):
- Re-read concealment mode and NS profile; if recognition is structurally absent (NS-profile confirms), note F3 as prerequisite; if concealment is operative, note relational/pastoral register

## Minimal-Pair Discriminators

**R2 vs. P4 (maieutic procedure):**
P4 is a sustained extended-questioning procedure for cases that require longer maieutic engagement. R2 is the single recognition-eliciting move that initiates that procedure when needed. R2 as a single tactic when the recognition is likely near the surface; P4 when the case needs an extended sequence of recognition-deepening questions before the recognition can be examined.

**R2 vs. V5 (directing attention to signs):**
V5 directs attention to external signs — creation, natural order. R2 directs attention to the internal recognitional movement — what arises in the interlocutor pre-argumentatively. R2 and V5 are complementary: R2 is the internal dimension, V5 the external. R2 first when the entry point is the interlocutor's own interior movement; V5 follows or accompanies.

**R2 vs. F3 (creating conditions for recognition):**
F3 creates conditions in which recognition can arise when it is not yet present. R2 elicits recognition that is already present but not yet consciously acknowledged. R2 when residual recognition is near the surface; F3 when the noetic structure must first be prepared before recognition can emerge.

**R2 vs. R3 (warranted basic belief):**
R2 is phenomenological — it elicits the recognition and holds space for it. R3 is analytical — it engages the interlocutor's philosophical account of what the recognition is (proper function, involuntariness, warrant). R2 precedes R3: recognition must first be elicited before the philosophical account of it can be engaged. Do not move to R3 before R2 has elicited something.

## Hold / Release Discipline

**Primary hold rule:** When recognition opens — when the interlocutor shows that something has moved — hold all argumentative content. Do not press for a conclusion. Do not ask "So you agree that God exists?" Do not move to the proper-function analysis. Leave space.

The hold is released by the interlocutor, not by the dāʿī. The interlocutor's own readiness to examine what has arisen is the release signal — not a timer, not the dāʿī's assessment that "enough time has passed."

**When argumentative content is re-engaged:** Only when the interlocutor has explicitly turned toward examination — "What does that mean?" "What do you think that is?" — or when recognition has been denied and the case has returned to a different register entirely.

**Do not press for conclusion:** This is the governing constraint of R2. The move that collapses what has opened — "So this proves God exists" — is the single most damaging anti-pattern in this register. Recognition at the moment of opening must be allowed to deepen on its own terms.

## Anti-Pattern Guard

**Pressing for conclusion when recognition opens:** The most damaging misuse of R2. When the interlocutor shows that something has moved, pressing immediately for a doctrinal conclusion ("So you believe in God?", "So God must exist?") collapses the space the recognition was opening. The recognition closes. What had opened is now defended against. This is not a minor stylistic error — it is a case-ending move in the wrong direction.

**Deploying R2 before criterion is cleared:** When the internalist criterion is still active, recognition elicited by R2 will be classified as psychological noise, not warrant. The criterion becomes the filter through which recognition is immediately interpreted away. R1 or E2 must challenge the criterion first so that recognition, when elicited, can register as recognition.
