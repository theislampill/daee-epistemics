# M9 — Predication-Mode Analysis

**Type:** Meta-tactic  
**Deploy when:** An argument's validity depends on a term carrying an identical sense across occurrences where the sense shifts; a question's coherence depends on a term applying to a domain where its meaning-conditions are not instantiated; an inference drawn by empirical-observational method is applied to a subject that exists only under conditions that method cannot access; the divine-attribute question of whether a term applies univocally, equivocally, or analogically is operative.

**Operates upstream of content-level response selection.** Before deciding what to say about a subject, M9 determines whether the predication structure of the claim — and the inferential structure of any argument about it — is well-formed. The four functions are distinct. Do not collapse them.

---

## Function 1 — Equivocation Exposure

**Trigger:** An argument's inference depends on a term carrying the same sense across two or more of its occurrences, but the sense of the term shifts between them.

Note: this trigger does not require the interlocutor to have raised a terminological objection. Equivocation is frequently invisible to both parties. M9-Function 1 is deployable by the dāʿī without the interlocutor having flagged the problem.

**Procedure:**
1. Identify the equivocating term.
2. State its sense in each occurrence explicitly — not loosely, but with sufficient precision to show that the two senses are not identical.
3. Show that the inference holds only if the senses are identical across all occurrences.
4. Show that they are not identical — the term has been allowed to shift.
5. Name what follows: the conclusion does not follow. The argument is invalid on its own terms, regardless of whether any individual premise is true.

**Terminal output: logical invalidity.** The argument fails structurally. The conclusion is not established. This is not a dispute about the truth of the premises; it is a diagnosis at the inference level.

**Worked example — tarkīb/iftiqār (attribute-composition and dependency):** The argument that
divine attributes entail composition (*tarkīb*) and dependency (*iftiqār*) uses four terms —
*tarkīb*, *ajzāʾ*, *iftiqār*, *ghayr* — as philosophical technical terms with meanings
specialized beyond their conventional Arabic senses. The setup employs the technical senses
(tarkīb = any entity describable by more than one predicate; ajzāʾ = any logically
distinguishable aspect; ghayr = any conceptually distinct feature; iftiqār = necessary
co-occurrence). The conclusion draws on the conventional senses (tarkīb = assembled from
separable pieces; ajzāʾ = ontologically separable components; ghayr = a separately existing
other; iftiqār = genuine causal need of one thing for a distinct other). Both the conventional
and technical readings produce locally true premises: under the technical sense, "an entity
qualified by a necessary concomitant attribute can only exist along with that attribute" is true
but tautological; under the conventional sense, "genuinely separable parts constitute a
dependency" is also true — but only applies where genuine separability obtains. The dependency
conclusion only follows under the conventional senses; but if the conventional senses were
operative throughout, the premise would need to be shown to be true of God's attributes, which
is precisely what is at issue. The argument requires the conventional senses in the conclusion
while doing its substantive work under the technical senses. This is the classical structure of
a concealed equivocation: neither reading makes the argument valid, but the shift between them
conceals the invalidity. Full treatment: `references/sound-reason-epistemology.md` §6.3.

**Distinction from M1:** M1 shows that the premises undermine the conclusion — each premise, if accepted, defeats the objection. M9-Function 1 is different: the premises may each be locally true under their respective readings. The failure is not in the premises self-destructing but in the inference depending on a term-identity that does not hold. A valid M9-Function 1 diagnosis does not require any premise to be false; it requires only that the inference be traced and exposed as unsupported by the shifted senses.

## Function 2 — Category Error / Domain-Boundary Diagnosis

**Trigger:** A term whose meaning-conditions are constituted within one ontological, legal, or social domain is applied, without adjustment, to a subject in a categorically different domain where those conditions are absent or not instantiated.

**Procedure:**
1. Identify the term and its home domain — the domain in which its meaning-conditions are constituted.
2. Enumerate the meaning-conditions that constitute the term's full sense in its home domain. These are not merely associated features but the constitutive conditions without which the term has no well-formed referent.
3. Show that those meaning-conditions are absent or non-instantiated in the target domain.
4. Conclude that the predication is malformed — the term does not acquire a referent in the target context by extension from its home domain. The question is not answered "no"; it does not yet have a determinate answer, because it has not yet achieved well-formedness.

**Terminal output: dissolution.** The question dissolves. This is explicitly different from refutation. Refutation answers a well-formed question negatively. Dissolution shows the question was not yet well-formed — it was asking for an answer in a context where the question's own constitutive conditions were not met.

**Worked example — theft applied to a pre-legal context:** The legal category of "theft" applied to a context predating the institution of property law and ownership conventions. "Theft" presupposes a legal framework within which ownership is established, a prohibition on taking what belongs to another, and a transgressive act within that constituted system. In a context where those institutions have not yet been constituted — where the category of ownership and its associated prohibitions are not yet in force — the term has no well-formed referent. The predication is malformed, not merely false. The question "was this theft?" does not await a yes/no answer; it dissolves because the meaning-conditions of the term are not instantiated in the target context. This is the structure of category-error dissolution: not refutation but dissolution.

## Function 2b — Ghayb-Boundary / Domain-of-Access Failure

**Trigger:** An inference drawn by empirical-observational method — evolutionary, statistical, historical-reconstructive, or coalescent-population-genetic — is applied to a subject that is accessible only through āthar (أثر — transmitted revelation), where the subject exists under conditions the empirical framework cannot model and has never been in contact with.

**Architecture:** Function 2b is upstream of Function 2. When both apply, establish method-jurisdiction failure first (Function 2b), then proceed to semantic dissolution (Function 2) if the specific predication still needs addressing after the jurisdiction failure is established. Do not begin with Function 2 and insert 2b afterward — 2b is prior in the diagnostic sequence.

**Procedure:**
1. Identify that the subject is a matter of ghayb (غيب — the unseen) — known only through what the āthar affirms, not through any empirical avenue.
2. Show that the inferential method being invoked operates on observable populations, processes, or phenomena; its domain of application is constituted by those observables.
3. Show that the subject (the originating conditions, the specific persons or events in question) was constituted under direct divine arrangement, entirely outside the populations and processes the empirical method models. The method was never pointed at this object.
4. Conclude: the method has no jurisdiction over this subject. Not that it gives the wrong answer — it gives no answer, because the subject is not within its domain of application. It is not a failure of measurement; it is a mismatch between instrument and object.

**Terminal output: method-jurisdiction failure.** Prior to and distinct from Function 2. Function 2 is semantic — the term's meaning-conditions do not transfer across the domain boundary. Function 2b is epistemic — the inferential instrument has no access to the subject regardless of what term is being used. Method-jurisdiction failure does not depend on any semantic analysis; it operates at the level of what the method can and cannot reach.

**The silence principle:** Where āthar is silent, silence is kept. Where āthar speaks, that is the limit of what can be affirmed about the subject. Empirical inference does not fill the silence — it is not calibrated to the subject the silence concerns.

**Worked example — statistical inference applied to a singular originating event:** Applying statistical inference about population distributions to a singular unrepeatable originating event. Population statistics operate on observed distributions across many instances of a repeatable type; the method's domain is constituted by that class of repeatable observables. A singular originating event — one that lies outside the class of repeatable observable instances the method models — is not within the method's domain. The method returns no verdict on it, not because the verdict is "unknown" but because the subject is outside the instrument's range entirely. This is method-jurisdiction failure: prior to any semantic question about what term applies to the subject, the inferential instrument has no access to it.

## Function 3 — Analogical Predication (Divine Attribute Subcase)

**Trigger:** A term is denied of God on the grounds that it cannot apply univocally (true), and therefore cannot apply at all (false inference); or a term is applied to God univocally, importing creaturely mode as if the semantic core required it.

**Procedure:** This subcase is fully treated in V8 (`references/techniques/V8-bila-kayf-anchor.md`) and `references/sound-reason-epistemology.md` §6. State the subcase explicitly and route to those files rather than duplicating their content here.

The load-bearing move is: terms carry a semantic core that applies genuinely across different instances while the mode of instantiation varies. The inference from "cannot apply univocally" to "cannot apply at all" is invalid — it treats univocal application as the only form of genuine application, which is unsupported. The tradition holds analogical (not univocal, not merely equivocal) predication, and both what carries across (the semantic core) and what does not (the creaturely mode) must be named in any response that deploys this subcase.

**Terminal output: positive analogical affirmation.** The term applies genuinely, with a different mode of instantiation. The affirmation is not empty.

**Routing:** Load V8 and §6. Do not generate fresh doctrine here.

For the attribute-multiplicity / tarkīb-iftiqār case specifically — where the shubhah
argues that possessing attributes entails composition and dependency — the positive
restoration step deploys *talāzum* (mutual necessitation) and *ṣifāt qāʾima bi-dhātihi*
(attributes subsisting in the essence) with the bilā kayf limit on mode. Full treatment
is in `references/sound-reason-epistemology.md` §6.3. That section cross-references this
function and gives the complete three-step response for that case.

## Distinctions from Adjacent Tactics

**M7 — The Definition Anchor:** M7 is deployed when an interlocutor challenges a word as a rhetorical move — consciously or not — to avoid engaging the underlying substance. The procedure is to anchor the definition and show the argument holds under any stipulated sense. M9 is different in three ways: (a) it handles predication failure as a logical structure, not as a rhetorical deflection; (b) it is frequently invisible to both parties and is diagnosed by the dāʿī rather than triggered by the interlocutor's challenge; (c) its terminal output is not "the argument holds under any definition" but "the argument fails structurally," or "the question is not yet well-formed," or "the method has no jurisdiction." M7 closes a rhetorical exit; M9 diagnoses a structural failure.

**M1 — Self-Refutation:** M1 shows that the premises of an argument, accepted, undermine the objection those premises were meant to support. M9-Function 1 shows that the inference fails even if each premise is locally true under its respective reading — a failure at the inference level, not at the premise level. M9-Function 1 does not require any premise to be false.

**M8 — Reductio:** M8 assumes the position and follows its consequences to show they are absurd or unacceptable. M9 diagnoses before following anywhere. M9 shows that the structure fails at the predication level — the term is not well-formed in the target domain, or the inference depends on an equivocation — prior to any consequence-tracing. M8 operates after the position has achieved enough structure to generate consequences; M9 operates before that.

**V8 — Bilā Kayf:** V8 is scoped to divine attribute language — transcendence, attribute-coherence, and philosopher's-God objections. M9 generalizes the same predication-mode insight across all domains. The divine-attribute subcase (M9-Function 3) is explicitly routed back to V8 rather than treated independently here. M9 does not duplicate V8; it provides the general framework within which V8 operates as a domain-specific application.
