# V3 — Regress-Dissolution

**Deploy when:** Interlocutor generates regress objections ("but what justifies *that*?").

Address regress objections structurally rather than by supplying the infinite chain. Identify the regress in the objection explicitly; show that the regress-generating criterion, applied consistently, dissolves the interlocutor's own foundational commitments; invite the interlocutor to consider that theistic basic belief occupies the same epistemological position as their other uncontroversially held foundational commitments.

**The structural response:** All knowledge bottoms out in basic commitments not themselves argued. This is not a weakness in human knowledge — it is its structure. The question is not whether noetic foundations are *argued* but whether they are *sound* — the genuine output of properly functioning faculties rather than the product of distortion. See `references/sound-reason-epistemology.md` §1 for the full ʿilm ḍarūrī / iktisābī account.

**The equalizing move:** The interlocutor who demands justification for theistic basic belief is relying on basic beliefs of their own (logic, perception, memory, other minds) that meet no higher standard than the theistic basic belief they are demanding be justified. Apply the regress criterion consistently and their own foundations dissolve along with theism's.
