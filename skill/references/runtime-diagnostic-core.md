# V1 - The Diagnostic Technique

## Role of V1 in the Skill

V1 is the entry gate. No tactic, technique, procedure, or case-library module is selected before V1 has produced a case-state. V1 does not answer the interlocutor; it produces the routing artifact that makes a matched answer possible.

## The Three Phases

### Phase 1 - Listening

Extended, non-argumentative engagement whose purpose is to map the interlocutor's actual noetic structure.

Track:

- what is held with the firmness of necessary knowledge
- what functions as the implicit doxastic rule
- what counts as evidence, warrant, and authority
- where affective weight sits
- what is conspicuously absent from the framework

### Phase 2 - Classification

Produce short codes on the main axes:

- NS code
- discourse orientation
- concealment
- deformation
- claim-type
- claim-level when a higher-order burden is visible or the full IR is being surfaced
- pattern profile when one recurring PF family is governing

Then run the mandatory passes in sequence:

1. `reason-disambiguation.md`
2. `foreign-premise-detection.md`
3. `prophetic-discourse-neutralization.md`
4. `arabic-backbone-predicates.md`

### Specialty markers to surface early

- `dalīl`, `wujūb al-naẓar`, or `taqlīd`-as-non-knowledge -> `kalamic-interlocutor.md`
- blank-slate or dual-nature `fiṭrah` language -> `fitrah-perspectives.md`
- attacks on necessary knowledge -> V9
- historical-critical, manuscript, canon, or text-history pressure -> V10 and the matched RT case
- prophetic discourse being redirected, semantically overridden, or evacuated -> `prophetic-discourse-neutralization.md`
- loaded negative theological terms (`jism`, `jihah`, `hadd`, `tarkib`, body, direction, place, limit, composition, substance) -> M9 before doctrinal release
- imported perfection / immutability / simplicity tribunal -> `perfection-criterion-usurpation.md`
- causal regress, simultaneous/successive series, circularity, or secondary-cause self-sufficiency -> `causal-series-taxonomy.md`
- public-language capture, silent redefinition, universals/particulars confusion, or mental/extra-mental confusion -> `definition-discipline.md`
- necessity/contingency proof-grammar pressure or philosophically trained proof-overreach -> `proof-method-audit.md`

### Phase 3 - Triage

Determine which instrument is the smallest matched next step, and in what order if multiple are required.

Use M5 as the deformation-triage subroutine inside V1, not as a rival opening architecture.

Compound-case sequencing rule:

- `ada` before `i'tiqadat mawrutha`
- `gharad` and `hawa` before intellectual content
- `i'tiqadat mawrutha` before evidence
- `zann` and `taqlid` in parallel where appropriate
- genuine `shubha` last

Cross-axis precedence:

- if DO-orient is non-truth-seeking, the next step is almost never a doctrinal case file
- if concealment is `irad`, argument is held until the matter has been allowed to press
- if concealment is `juhud` or `istikbar`, argument will not land
- if reason-category or foreign-premise detection makes V2 mandatory, content waits
- if semantic neutralization or a lexical-ontological trap is live, doctrinal content waits behind the semantic gate

## The Case-State Output

V1 terminates in a composed line that feeds `case-state-schema.md`:

```text
Case: <family>    Claim: <type> [@ <level>]    Pattern: <PF-X | none>    NS: NS-X [| NS-Y]    DO-orient: <code>    Concealment: <code>    RT (if applicable): <RT-X>    Deformation: <primary> [| <secondary>]    Strength: <strong | provisional | low>    Next: <smallest matched module>
```

Omit `@ <level>` only when the case is routine first-order after higher-order triggers
have been checked and found inactive.

Where legibility is needed, surface the fuller `[Case State]` block from `case-state-schema.md`.

## Critical Warning

Presenting intellectual content to someone whose governing barrier is vested interest, entrenched will, identity-performance, semantic neutralization, or a loaded lexical trap does not merely fail; it gives the barrier new material to organize around.

## Downstream Ordering Rule

Once V1 has produced the case-state and M5 has confirmed the governing deformation, run M1 or M1-P first among downstream moves whenever self-refutation is actually present.

## When to Re-Run V1

Re-run or update the case-state whenever:

- a move clears an upstream barrier and exposes a new downstream issue
- the interlocutor shifts register
- affective tone shifts significantly
- a new objection family appears
- the case becomes genuinely mixed

# Noetic Reading Checklist — Full Treatment

## What a Noetic Structure Is

A subject's noetic structure is the ontology of their belief-world: the ordered configuration
of commitments, categories, inferential norms, testimonial attitudes, interpretive filters,
background assumptions, and belief-relations by which they apprehend, organize, warrant,
resist, and sustain judgments about reality.

It is not what a subject would say their epistemology is, or what they would list as their
beliefs. It is the operative configuration — discursively inferable from speech, reasoning,
categories, premises, interpretive habits, and stated motives. A subject whose stated
epistemology is "I follow the evidence wherever it leads" may have a noetic structure that
pre-filters what counts as evidence, blocks certain inferential moves, and holds a specific
set of worldview commitments as invisible foundations. The stated epistemology and the
operative noetic structure can be substantially different. The analysis targets the operative
structure.

## When to Run This Checklist

- Whenever a personal ʿaqīdah or worldview is presented for diagnosis
- Before any sustained engagement — the checklist reveals which instrument has traction
- When a move clears an upstream barrier and exposes a new downstream issue, or when the interlocutor shifts register in a way that changes intervention order
- When the interlocutor's position appears internally inconsistent

## What This Checklist Should Produce

Convert findings into a compact case state rather than a lecture. At minimum, determine:

- the primary noetic issue that governs the next move
- the likely deformation or deformation pair
- whether the case is strong, provisional, or low-confidence
- which tactic, technique, procedure, or case module becomes the smallest matched next step
- whether a pseudo-neutral slogan is installing an imported tribunal, denying a worldview while
  carrying a hidden ʿaqīdah, signaling concealment possibility, and requiring bounded deployment
- what missing differentiator would change the read

### Checklist Rider - Pseudo-Neutral Slogans

When slogans such as "I just follow the evidence," "I'm neutral," "I'm just following
reason," or "I looked and just wasn't convinced" appear, do not classify only the
proposition-level content. Classify the meta-noetic posture:

- imported tribunal / criterion-smuggling
- pseudo-neutral reason claim
- worldview denial / hidden ʿaqīdah
- concealment possibility
- deployment discipline: let the stronger present cue govern; ask one bounded diagnostic
  question first; if the same blocker stays live, add only minimal tribunal-clearing; then
  pause rather than jumping to full discharge

## The Nine Analytical Dimensions

Run internally. Present findings, not the checklist itself.

**Dimension 1 — Basic vs. derivative:**
What does the subject treat as foundational — not inferred from anything else? Specifically:
- What propositions do they hold with the firmness of ʿilm ḍarūrī (necessary knowledge)?
- What derived philosophical conclusions have been elevated to foundational status without
  examination? The naturalist who treats causal closure of the physical as a basic given
  is treating an inferential conclusion as if it were directly perceived.
- What is the actual order of warrant — what genuinely grounds what?

*Theoretical grounding: `references/sound-reason-epistemology.md` §1.*

**Dimension 2 — Evidence, warrant, authority, testimony, defeater, explanation:**
Surface the implicit criteria:
- What counts as evidence for this person? (Empirically testable only? Or does the full
  range — perception, testimony, reason, inner experience — qualify?)
- What would they accept as warranting a belief? What would defeat a belief they hold?
- What counts as authority? (Peer-reviewed consensus only? Or does transmitted testimony
  through reliable chains qualify?)
- What would count as an explanation? (Efficient-causal mechanism only? Or does
  teleological, rational, and personal explanation qualify?)
- Are these criteria applied consistently across theistic and non-theistic questions?

**Dimension 3 — Inferential norms:**
- What inferential moves does the subject permit, deploy, deny, or treat as absent?
- Are they applying different inferential standards to different domains?
- Where are the inferential norms unstable — where does the subject make moves they
  would disallow in others' arguments?
- What would follow from their stated inferential norms if applied consistently?

*Theoretical grounding: `references/sound-reason-epistemology.md` §1 (ʿilm iktisābī and its
dependence on ḍarūrī foundations).*

**Dimension 4 — Worldview commitments governing interpretation:**
- What creedal or philosophical commitments are functioning as interpretive filters —
  shaping what can be seen, not only what is concluded?
- These are typically invisible to the subject. They feel like common sense, not
  like chosen positions. Name them.
- The commitment to materialism-as-default means every anomalous datum is interpreted
  as a problem to dissolve, not evidence to weigh. The commitment to naturalism means
  consciousness, moral facts, and mathematical objects must all be accounted for within
  the physical — not considered as potential evidence against it.
- Identify the governing commitment before engaging its outputs.

*Theoretical grounding: `references/sound-reason-epistemology.md` §2 (proper function — when
faculties are aimed at truth vs. filtered by survival or social commitments).*

**Dimension 5 — Foreign categories and parasitic assumptions:**
- Has the subject imported categories from a framework that does not belong to the
  question being examined?
- Classic forms: applying the standards of natural science to metaphysical questions;
  applying modern liberal political categories to classical theological questions;
  using psychological categories ("projection," "cognitive bias") to pre-empt
  epistemological evaluation; importing Kantian epistemological categories into a
  context that does not share Kant's premises.
- Name what has been imported, from where, and why it does not belong.

**Dimension 6 — Relations among premises, conclusions, and modes of assent:**
- Does the subject hold their conclusions with firmness proportioned to their premises?
- Where is certainty overclaimed on conjectural premises?
- Where is a tentative or unexamined premise doing load-bearing work?
- Where does the subject hold a conclusion with far more firmness than any chain of
  reasoning from their stated premises could produce?
- Are there commitments held with the character of ʿilm ḍarūrī that are actually
  ʿilm iktisābī — acquired, not necessary?

*Theoretical grounding: `references/sound-reason-epistemology.md` §1.*

**Dimension 7 — Contradictions, absences, instabilities, performative self-undermining:**
- Where do the subject's commitments conflict with each other?
- What is conspicuously absent — a grounding for moral commitments, an account of
  consciousness, an explanation for why reason is reliable, a basis for the subject's
  own epistemic confidence?
- Where does the discourse undermine itself in the act of being produced? (See M1-P,
  performative self-refutation, in `references/tactics/M1P-performative-self-refutation.md`.)
- Where do the subject's stated positions commit them to conclusions they manifestly
  reject? (See M8, reductio, in `references/tactics/M8-reductio.md`.)

**Dimension 8 — Discourse orientation:**
See `references/diagnostics/discourse-orientation.md` for full treatment. In brief:
- Is the discourse ordered toward truth, warrant, and disciplined judgment?
- Or toward identity-performance, novelty-seeking/autotelic stimulation, or conjecture
  without anchor?
- This determines whether intellectual engagement is the right instrument at all.

**Dimension 9 — Distance from the point of yaqīn:**
Synthesizing diagnostic across all eight dimensions:
- How far is the subject's noetic structure from the point where sound reason and
  authentic revelation fully coincide?
- Convergence indicators: stable, consistent conclusions on foundational matters;
  central claims recognizable across independent cultural traditions; involuntary
  recognition in states of genuine attentiveness; agreement with what unimpaired
  cognizers universally hold.
- Divergence indicators: proliferating internal disagreement among the framework's
  own adherents; inability to agree on foundational matters; conclusions that require
  systematic suspension of ordinary cognition; the need for increasing theoretical
  complexity to maintain positions against intuitive resistance.
- The divergence pattern is not just empirically interesting — it is theoretically
  significant. Tawātur fiṭrī establishes that frameworks far from truth will exhibit
  this pattern. The philosopher who disagrees with other philosophers even in
  astronomy is showing what happens when departing from the point of yaqīn.

*Theoretical grounding: `references/sound-reason-epistemology.md` §4 (tawātur as final epistemic
   guarantor); the divergence diagnostic.*

## What to Do with the Findings

The findings from the checklist are not all presented to the subject. Present what is
useful; withhold what would be experienced as a lecture or an accusation. Typically:

- Dimension 1 findings → select whether E-register, R-register, or V2 is needed first
- Dimension 2 findings → inform E1 (broadening evidential scope) if applicable
- Dimension 3 findings → inform V3 (regress-dissolution) or V9 (necessary knowledge
  priority) if applicable
- Dimension 4 findings → V4 (contamination-identification) or V2 (reconstituting reason)
- Dimension 5 findings → V4; also relevant to M7 (definition anchor) if foreign
  categories are creating terminological confusion
- Dimension 6 findings → M2 (prior probability probe) or M8 (reductio)
- Dimension 7 findings → M1 (self-refutation), M1-P (performative self-refutation),
  M3 (orphaned intuition probe), or M8 (reductio)
- Dimension 8 findings → see discourse-orientation.md; may mean suspending intellectual
  engagement entirely
- Dimension 9 findings → frame response: how close or far is the subject from what is
  recoverable through honest inquiry alone?

## Strength-of-Read Guide

- `Strong`: multiple dimensions converge on the same upstream issue and intervention path.
- `Provisional`: the likely intervention is visible, but one or more routing dimensions remain open.
- `Low confidence`: the checklist can only classify the claim-type, not the whole case.

If the diagnosis remains low confidence, route through `mixed-case-handling.md` before
claiming a settled read.

## Thin-Basis / Excerpt Mode

When the basis is only a slogan, short excerpt, or one-turn prompt:

**Minimum-basis rule before confident single NS emission:**

All three of the following dimensions must carry independent convergent signal before a single NS code may be emitted with confidence:

1. **Dimension 1** — What the subject treats as basic or non-inferential is discernible, not merely assumed from surface vocabulary.
2. **Dimension 4** — The governing worldview commitment acting as an interpretive filter is discernible, not inferred by pattern-match to a stereotype.
3. **Dimension 8** — The discourse orientation (truth-seeking vs. identity-performance vs. autotelic vs. conjecture) is discernible from the input.

If any of these three dimensions is under-signalled, the NS code is provisional, not confirmed.

**Fields that must remain provisional until basis is confirmed:**

- `NS code` — emit as `NS-X | NS-Y` or `NS-?` when fewer than all three dimensions carry convergent signal
- `Deformation type` — do not commit to a primary deformation when only surface language is available
- `Concealment mode` — do not assign a specific concealment mode from a single utterance
- `Discourse orientation` — mark as `underdetermined` when the input does not distinguish truth-seek from identity-performance or autotelic

**Decisive missing differentiator: mandatory, not optional.**

When any of the three minimum-basis dimensions is under-signalled, the `Decisive missing differentiator` field must be populated. It is not optional. State explicitly what additional signal would resolve the ambiguity — this is both a self-audit guard and a routing instruction for the next exchange.

**Pattern-profile as substitute diagnosis:**

If the recurring case family is clearer than the noetic profile, surface a `pattern_profile` from `pattern-profiling.md` while keeping the NS read provisional. Routing to the right family is often more tractable from a thin excerpt than assigning a complete noetic profile.

**Failure test:** Excerpt mode did not govern the response if a single NS code was emitted confidently from a slogan or one-turn excerpt without independent convergent signal across Dimensions 1, 4, and 8 — or if the decisive missing differentiator was omitted because the response felt complete without it.

Excerpt mode does not suspend diagnosis. It suspends overcommitment.

# Seven Deformations of the Fiṭrah — Full Treatment

## Preamble

The seven deformations are not a typology of false beliefs. They are specific cognitive-moral
mechanisms — each with its own causal structure, its own phenomenology, and its own required
intervention. Misidentifying which is operative is the most common reason epistemological
conversations fail. Deploying the wrong instrument does not merely fail to help; it can
actively worsen the situation.

The critical diagnostic question is always: what is *actually* preventing recognition? Not
what the interlocutor presents as preventing recognition, but what is actually operating.
Most interlocutors present their barrier as shubhah (deformation 7) — the only apparently
respectable one. The diagnostic task is to determine whether shubhah is genuinely operative
or whether it is covering one of the other six.

The seven are treated here as a standing sound-reason diagnostic of why recognition gets
blocked. They are not merely metaphors but pre-specified conditions examinable in the
particular case.

---

## Routing Summary

| Deformation | Primary cue | First move | Do not do first | Common confusion |
|-------------|-------------|------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha | Filter treated as common sense | V2 | Present first-order evidence through the filter | Taqlīd or ẓann without framework-level filtering |
| Mushābara fāsida (1-A) | One premise regenerates the downstream conclusions | Name the premise, then apply M1 to the premise itself | Keep arguing derivative conclusions while the premise stands | Systemic inherited filtering |
| Hawā | Objections multiply as soon as one clears | F2 or relational engagement | Supply more arguments | Genuine shubhah |
| Ẓann | Position feels examined but is inherited by default | V7 | Treat it as deep principled skepticism | Taqlīd |
| Taqlīd | Position adopted by imitation | Invite taḥqīq | Shame the person for inherited belief | Ẓann |
| ʿĀda | Distortion feels like direct reality | V2 then V5 | Argue as if the signs are already landing | Iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha |
| Gharaḍ | Honest inquiry feels costly | F2 | Force argumentative escalation | Hawā |
| Shubhah | The apparent barrier actually yields to intellectual clearing | V9 or matched doctrinal/case response | Attribute hidden motive too quickly | Any of the other six wearing a respectable surface |

## 1. Iʿtiqādāt Mawrūtha — Inherited Beliefs

**Mechanism:** Doctrines absorbed from one's intellectual or cultural environment without
examination, now functioning as invisible filters on what can be perceived, counted as
evidence, or considered as a legitimate conclusion. The filter is invisible because it has
been internalized as common sense rather than recognized as a chosen position.

**Phenomenology:** The interlocutor experiences their filtering as simply "what reason
says." The naturalist who holds that only empirically testable propositions count does not
experience this as a philosophical commitment — they experience it as the obvious
requirement for serious inquiry. The filter pre-empts rather than overrides.

**Common forms:**
- Scientism (only empirically testable propositions are epistemically acceptable)
- Methodological naturalism elevated to metaphysical naturalism
- Historical-critical hermeneutics applied to revelation as if this were simply "reading
  carefully"
- Liberal secular assumptions about individual autonomy as the organizing value of ethics

**Why intellectual content alone does not help initially:** Until the filter is named and
loosened, evidence and argument are processed through it and emerge as confirming the
interlocutor's existing position. The filter does not fail gracefully — it absorbs.

**Intervention:** V2 (Reconstituting the Conception of Reason) first, always. Name the
framework as historically contingent, show it is not self-grounding, show it is not applied
consistently. The goal is not refutation but loosening — creating enough distance between
the interlocutor and the filter that they can examine it rather than looking through it.

---

### 1-A. Mushābara Fāsida — Faulty Presupposition (Subsection of Deformation 1)

**The distinction from systemic filtering:** Iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha operates as a pervasive
framework — a worldview that filters what can be counted as evidence or reached as a
conclusion across the board. Mushābara fāsida is different in kind: a *single* foundational
assumption, held with the firmness of necessity, that is actually false — and that distorts
everything built on it. The interlocutor may be reasoning in complete good faith, without
any hawā or gharaḍ, and yet every step takes them further from clarity because the starting
point is wrong.

**Why the distinction matters operationally:** Against iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha, the intervention
is framework-level (V2): loosen the entire inherited conception of reason. Against mushābara
fāsida, the intervention is surgical: identify the specific false premise and address it
directly rather than engaging the conclusions it has generated. Engaging conclusions while
the premise remains untouched achieves nothing — the premise will generate the conclusions
again. The dāʿī must work upstream.

**How to identify it:** Ask: is there one foundational claim — typically held as self-evident
and not examined — from which the interlocutor's other positions follow necessarily? If
addressing a derived conclusion produces agreement but no movement (the interlocutor re-derives
the same conclusion from the same premise), the premise is mushābara fāsida.

**Common forms:**
- "The only things that exist are things that could in principle be detected by scientific
  instruments" — held as a definition of reality, not a claim about it. Everything follows.
- "Morality is entirely constructed by social agreement" — held as given; the conclusion that
  God-belief is merely a cultural artifact follows automatically.
- "Whatever cannot be understood is not real" — the premise that divine transcendence means
  non-existence follows necessarily, regardless of what evidence is offered.
- "Extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence" — held as an axiom of neutral inquiry;
  in fact a contestable prior probability assignment being smuggled in as methodology.

**The upstream intervention:**
1. *Identify the premise explicitly.* Name it as a premise, not as a fact: "You are treating
   X as self-evident. But that is itself a claim — where does it come from and what grounds it?"
2. *Show it is not self-grounding.* The premise cannot establish its own truth by the standards
   it sets. This is the M1 move applied to the premise, not to the argument it generates.
3. *Show what follows if the premise is released.* Do not argue that the premise is false —
   show that if it were examined rather than assumed, the entire downstream architecture would
   require rebuilding. The goal is to make the premise visible and contingent, not to win a
   point.
4. *Do not engage the downstream conclusions until step 3 is complete.* Every conclusion the
   interlocutor has reached is a product of the premise. Arguing with the conclusions while
   the premise stands is building on the contested ground the premise occupies.

**Distinguishing from iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha in practice:** In compound cases both are often
present. The rule: if loosening the general framework (V2) produces movement across the board,
the dominant deformation is iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha. If V2 produces general movement but one
specific conclusion remains immovable and generates its own resistance, look for the faulty
presupposition that anchors it specifically.

**Connection to the regress-dissolution technique (V3):** When an interlocutor presses a
regress — "but what grounds that?" — the presupposition being protected is often the actual
target. V3 dissolves the regress; identifying mushābara fāsida identifies *why* the regress
was being pressed in the first place.

## 2. Hawā — Entrenched Bias

**Mechanism:** The will has dug in against a conclusion it does not want. The position is
held not because of argument but because the cost of revision is too high — psychologically,
relationally, or existentially. The will leads; the intellect follows and provides
rationalizations.

**Phenomenology:** From the outside, hawā looks like intellectual resistance — the
interlocutor seems to have objections. From the inside (when the interlocutor is honest),
it feels like a kind of resistance that is not quite intellectual — a sense that accepting
the conclusion would cost something that cannot be paid.

**Distinguishing from genuine shubhah:** Genuine shubhah is responsive to intellectual
engagement — the objection, once properly dissolved, loses its force. Hawā is not. When
objections are dissolved, new ones appear. The pattern of accelerating objection-generation
is the diagnostic signal: this is not genuine intellectual inquiry but a will looking for
cover.

**Why intellectual content deepens resistance:** Each new argument the dāʿī provides gives
hawā new material to organize around. The interlocutor uses the increased complexity as
further evidence that the matter is genuinely unclear. More content makes things worse.

**Intervention:** Relational engagement before any argument. F2 (foregrounding the volitional
dimension) — gently, without accusation: "Is this an intellectual objection, or is there
something personally at stake in the question?" The V1 listening phase is essential. The
goal is to create enough trust and enough acknowledgment of what is actually at stake that
genuine inquiry becomes available.

---

## 3. Ẓann — Unreflective Conjecture

**Mechanism:** Operating on assumed-by-default positions that are treated as settled but
have never been genuinely examined. The position has the phenomenological texture of a
considered conclusion but the epistemic status of an unexamined assumption.

**Phenomenology:** The interlocutor feels they have "thought about it" — but what they have
done is absorb a position from their milieu and give it the label of having been considered.
Atheism or skepticism absorbed from an intellectual community without genuine investigation
is the paradigm case. The interlocutor who says "I've never found the arguments convincing"
may never have genuinely examined them.

**Distinguishing from iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha:** Iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha is about invisible
filtering frameworks. Ẓann is about position-conclusions held as examined when they are
not. They often co-occur.

**Intervention:** V7 (the taqlīd check) — the symmetric form: distinguish taqlīd from
taḥqīq (genuine investigation); apply the check to the interlocutor's skepticism with
the same force one would apply it to inherited religious belief. "Have you actually
investigated this, or is this simply what people in your milieu conclude?"

---

## 4. Taqlīd — Blind Imitation

**Mechanism:** Holding a position through uncritical imitation of one's intellectual
environment. Getting to truth (or error) by chance — the position happens to be right
or wrong, but the path to it was imitation, not inquiry.

**Distinguishing from ẓann:** Ẓann is about holding a conclusion as examined when it is
not. Taqlīd is about the mode of acquisition — the position was adopted by following
others, not by investigation. They often overlap.

**The symmetric application:** This deformation applies with equal force to inherited
religious belief. A believer who holds Islam because they were born into a Muslim family,
who has never genuinely examined the grounds of their belief, is holding it by taqlīd
just as much as the skeptic holds their skepticism by taqlīd. The dāʿī who challenges
the interlocutor's taqlīd must themselves hold their own position by taḥqīq — genuine
investigation. See `references/tactics/symmetric-taqlid-check.md`.

**Intervention:** Distinguish taqlīd from taḥqīq; invite actual inquiry. The tradition
asks genuine investigation of everyone — including the believer. This is not a concession
that weakens the position; it is the tradition's own epistemological demand.

---

## 5. ʿĀda — Habituated Pattern

**Mechanism:** Cognitive and behavioral habits that have become second nature, displacing
the original deliverances of the fiṭrah. The distorted framework no longer feels like a
distortion — it feels like reality. What was once an acquired filter has become the
apparent texture of experience itself.

**Phenomenology:** Unlike iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha (which is about intellectual filters),
ʿāda operates at the level of immediate experience. The person for whom atheism has
become habituated does not experience theistic signs as evidence that is outweighed by
other evidence — they simply do not experience them as signs at all. The āyāt pass
without resonance.

**Distinguishing from iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha:** Iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha is an intellectual
framework filtering what counts as evidence. ʿĀda is a habituated cognitive pattern
filtering what is even apprehended. ʿĀda is deeper — it operates earlier in the
perceptual/cognitive chain.

**Why this is the hardest deformation to address:** The filter is not available for
intellectual examination because it shapes what is available to examine. Naming the
framework (V2) helps with iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha but does not reach ʿāda directly — the
habituated pattern must first be interrupted.

**Intervention:** V2 + V5 in sequence. V2 first: name the habituated framework and
create cognitive distance from it. Then V5: direct attention to signs, non-inferentially,
while the distance created by V2 is still active. The window between loosening the habit
and its reestablishment is the moment when āyāt can land. Spiritual practice (F3) is
the long-term intervention: practices that disrupt habituated patterns and restore
attentiveness to what the fiṭrah was built to apprehend.

---

## 6. Gharaḍ — Vested Interest

**Mechanism:** Something is genuinely at stake in the question — social belonging,
professional identity, relationship, lifestyle, self-concept — that makes honest inquiry
feel threatening. Honest inquiry would produce a conclusion the interlocutor cannot
afford to reach without paying a cost they are not willing to pay.

**Phenomenology:** Gharaḍ is often not consciously recognized as such. The interlocutor
experiences their resistance as intellectual. Only in moments of genuine self-reflection
does the real structure become visible: "I cannot believe this because of what it would
cost me."

**Distinguishing from hawā:** Hawā is the will dug in against a conclusion because it
does not *want* it. Gharaḍ is the will protecting a stake — something external that
would be lost. They co-occur frequently. The distinction matters for the intervention:
hawā needs acknowledgment of the volitional barrier; gharaḍ needs acknowledgment of
what is actually at stake.

**Why intellectual content is counterproductive:** The interlocutor who faces gharaḍ is
not genuinely open to inquiry. More intellectual content increases the cost — it brings
the threatening conclusion closer. Each clear argument makes the interlocutor more
defensive, not less.

**Intervention:** F2 (foregrounding the volitional dimension): ask what is actually at
stake. This must be done with genuine care — not as an accusation but as pastoral
attention. "What would it cost you, practically, if this were true?" Relational
engagement precedes and conditions everything else. Until the cost is acknowledged and
held with some honesty, no intellectual engagement is productive.

---

## 7. Shubhah — Spurious Objection

**Mechanism:** A specific intellectual impediment lodged in the mind — a puzzle, a
pointed objection, a counter-argument that has not been dissolved — that is blocking
access to what the fiṭrah would otherwise apprehend. Unlike the other six deformations,
this one operates purely at the intellectual level.

**The critical distinction:** Shubhah is the *only* deformation that responds directly
to intellectual engagement. This is why it must be accurately identified: engaging a
genuine shubhah with intellectual content is appropriate and productive. Engaging one
of the other six with intellectual content is not.

**What makes a shubhah genuine:** The objection has logical force that has not been
dissolved. The interlocutor is genuinely stuck — not protecting a stake, not imitating
their milieu, not operating through a habituated filter — but facing a problem they
cannot see past. When the shubhah is dissolved, the recognition it was blocking becomes
available.

**What makes a shubhah spurious (in the broader sense):** Most presented objections are
not genuine shubuhāt. They are covers for one of the other six. The tell: if addressing
the objection carefully and completely produces not relief but a new objection, the
barrier was never intellectual.

**The shubhah as disease of the heart (waswās):** The tradition distinguishes between
a shubhah that arises from genuine inquiry and one that functions as waswās — whispered
doubt, a disease of the heart that casts doubt on what is firmly known. The latter does
not respond to intellectual engagement because its purpose is destabilization, not
resolution. Name it as such when it appears.

**Intervention:** First, determine whether genuine or covering another deformation. If
genuine: engage it directly and fully. Apply M1 (self-refutation) if the objection's
premises undermine it; apply M8 (reductio) if following the objection to its conclusion
produces an unacceptable result; apply V9 (necessary knowledge over discursive reasoning)
if the objection's conclusion contradicts a universally held fiṭrī intuition. If not
genuine: address the underlying deformation; do not engage the intellectual surface.

---

### Minimal-Pair Discriminator: Hawā vs. Gharaḍ

*Decision rule:* Is the will's resistance general (it does not want this conclusion) or specific (it has something particular at stake externally that would be lost)?

*Left correct (hawā):* Interlocutor resists theistic conclusions across every register and form of engagement. When one objection is dissolved, another appears immediately. The resistance feels totalizing — not "I cannot believe because of my job" but a generalized sense that belief would cost something existential but unnameable. The will is dug in without an identifiable external stake. Deformation is hawā. Correct response: F2 (foregrounding the volitional dimension) without forcing identification of a specific stake; relational engagement; no additional intellectual content.

*Right correct (gharaḍ):* Interlocutor has a clearly identifiable stake: professional identity as a secular academic, a relationship that would be strained, community membership that depends on the skeptical position, or a lifestyle incompatible with what belief would require. The resistance is not totalizing — it is organized around a specific cost. "I can see why you'd say that, but I just can't..." followed by deflection toward the specific stake. Deformation is gharaḍ. Correct response: F2 directed at naming the specific stake; "What would it cost you, practically, if this were true?" The question must be pastoral, not accusatory.

*Differentiating signal:* Ask internally: can I name the specific external thing being protected? If yes, and if the interlocutor's resistance maps to that specific thing, gharaḍ governs. If the resistance is more diffuse — distributed across all potential conclusions rather than organized around a specific stake — hawā governs. In compound cases (both present), gharaḍ is addressed first because naming the specific stake often uncovers the hawā underneath it; gharaḍ without hawā is relatively rare.

---

### Minimal-Pair Discriminator: Hawā vs. Shubhah

*Decision rule:* Does the barrier yield to intellectual clearing, or does it generate new objections when existing ones are addressed?

*Left correct (hawā):* Interlocutor presents objections that, when addressed clearly and completely, are replaced immediately by new objections. The pattern of objection-generation accelerates as the engagement deepens. No single dissolved objection produces relief or even temporary recognition — only more material. The intellectual surface is genuine, but the governing structure is volitional. Deformation is hawā. Correct response: Do not supply more arguments. Identify the pattern explicitly (F2) or shift to relational engagement. More intellectual content gives the resistance more material to organize around.

*Right correct (shubhah):* Interlocutor presents an objection that, when addressed clearly and completely, produces visible relief or genuine re-examination. The objection had actual intellectual force that was blocking access to what the fiṭrah would otherwise apprehend. The person was not protecting a conclusion — they were genuinely stuck. Dissolution of the objection produces movement, not new objections in the same pattern. Deformation is shubhah. Correct response: Engage directly and fully; apply M1, M8, or V9 as matched; stop after dissolution.

*Differentiating signal:* The accelerating-objection pattern is the primary signal for hawā. If objections multiply and shift as soon as one is dissolved — especially if the new objections concern different families (one round about evil, the next about evolution, the next about history) — the governing structure is not intellectual. A genuine shubhah stays focused on one point and yields (at least partially) when that point is addressed. Secondary signal: affect. Hawā often carries a kind of urgency or emotional loading inconsistent with pure intellectual puzzlement; genuine shubhah has a quality of intellectual frustration rather than volitional resistance.

---

### Minimal-Pair Discriminator: Shubhah vs. Grief-Register (M4)

*Decision rule:* Is the problem of evil being presented as a propositional argument with identifiable premises, or is it being expressed as moral protest, grief, or a felt injustice that has not been translated into argument form?

*Left correct (shubhah):* Interlocutor presents the logical problem of evil with explicit premises: "An all-powerful, all-knowing, all-good God would prevent suffering. Suffering exists. Therefore no such God exists." The form is argumentative; the pressure is propositional. Deformation is shubhah (if genuine). Correct response: M1 check, then M2 or DO-2 matched content.

*Right correct (grief / M4):* Interlocutor's child died and they cannot reconcile a loving God with the loss. They do not offer a syllogism; they express that the loss makes God seem absent or indifferent. The form is grief and moral protest. The deformation is not shubhah — it is a relational wound that has been given theological language. Correct response: M4 register (grief/moral protest); not DO-2 probabilistic engagement.

*Differentiating signal:* Does the presentation contain identifiable premises that could be evaluated and potentially falsified? If yes, shubhah may be operative. If the presentation is narrative, affective, and relational — structured around a personal story of loss or moral outrage rather than a logical structure — grief register governs. Note: grief and shubhah can co-occur; when they do, grief must be addressed before the shubhah is engaged.

## The Compound Case

> **Canonical source for compound-case sequencing.** All other files (SKILL.md,
> V1-diagnostic.md, M5-deformation-triage.md, individual NS profile opening-move
> hierarchies in `case-library/profiles/`, P1-fitrah-restoration.md) refer to this section rather than
> restating the rules. If an apparent conflict arises between files, this section
> governs.

Most real interlocutors exhibit more than one deformation. The intervention must sequence
correctly. The rule is always *outside in* — the layer that most governs access to the
next layer is addressed first.

1. ʿĀda, if present, must be addressed before iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha can be reached — the
   habituated pattern blocks the filter from being examined.
2. Gharaḍ and hawā, if present, must be acknowledged before any intellectual content —
   the volitional barrier will absorb intellectual content as more material for
   resistance.
3. Iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha must be loosened before evidence can land — the framework
   pre-filters what will be counted as evidence.
4. Ẓann and taqlīd can often be addressed in parallel with iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha — both
   respond to V7 and symmetric-taqlīd checks that compose naturally with framework
   loosening.
5. Mushābara fāsida, when present alongside iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha, requires surgical
   targeting of the specific premise in addition to framework loosening — the premise
   will regenerate the conclusions even after the framework has been named.
6. Shubhah, if genuine, is addressed last — after the framework is cleared. A shubhah
   engaged while upstream filters govern the case is a shubhah engaged at the wrong
   level; the interlocutor will experience the response as confirmation that the
   filter was operative.

**Register-hold rule:** The sequence above describes deformation order. The concealment
mode and DO-orient may additionally hold the entire deformation intervention pending a
register shift. If DO-orient is `identity-perf`, `autotelic`, or `zann-mode`, or if
concealment is `irad`, `juhud`, or `istikbar`, the matched deformation instrument waits on
the register shift. (Under `irad`, the deformation layer often cannot even be read with
confidence — the subject has not engaged enough for the deformation to surface.)
Deformation sequencing is *within* the register that permits intellectual or maieutic
engagement; it does not overrule register.

**Operationalization:** `references/tactics/M5-deformation-triage.md` provides the
pairwise table that applies this sequence move-by-move. V1 Phase 3 (triage) calls M5;
M5 reads this section. The rules here are the standard; the table there is the
application.

The practitioner who attempts to engage shubhah while gharaḍ and iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha are
unaddressed is working at the wrong level. The layers must be addressed from the outside in.

## 8. Theological Grounding — Kufr, Cognitive Dissonance, and Mentalizing Deficits

The seven deformations describe cognitive-moral mechanisms. Islamic theology provides the
underlying account of why some denial is morally culpable and some is not. Three categories
explain why people hold worldviews diverging from the fiṭrah's deliverances:

**Category A — Limited data and environment (ẓann, taqlīd, iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha)**

The first cause of divergent worldviews is simply the amount and quality of data the person
has had access to. People form beliefs on the basis of their experience, environment, and
what has been transmitted to them. Given the diversity of environments and the limits of
any human life, divergent beliefs are not surprising. This corresponds to deformations 1–4
(iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha, ẓann, taqlīd, and initial ʿāda). The person in this category bears
relatively low culpability — the prophetic mission exists precisely to address initial
corruption through the delivery of clear guidance.

**Category B — Willful suppression (hawā, gharaḍ)**

The second cause is cognitive dissonance: the willful suppression of what a person knows
or recognizes to be true, for reasons of emotional or material gain (social belonging,
economic status, political identity, personal comfort). The Qurʾānic root *k-f-r* (كفر) —
literally "to cover" — indexes exactly this structure: covering recognition rather than
lacking it. The Qurʾān frequently describes those who reject the truth as *believing they
are doing right* (2:11-12) — they are not ignorant; they have reconfigured their framework
so that their suppression looks, to themselves, like an honest position.

**Sharʿī vs. lexical use.** The full sharʿī category of *kufr* — theological unbelief
standing over against *īmān* — has its own conditions (knowledge, capacity, absence of
excusing factors, the distinction between the act and the actor, and the reserved
jurisdiction over takfīr of the particular person). This skill does *not* adjudicate that
theological judgment. What it uses is the *lexical-analytic* force of the root: the
deformation map and the concealment map both trade on the root-sense *covering* to
describe what has happened inside a subject's noetic structure. A diagnostic emission of
`gharad` or `hawa` on the deformation axis — or `juhud` on the concealment axis — names a
cognitive-moral mechanism; it is not, on its own, a pronouncement of takfīr. See
`references/terminology.md` for the full treatment of the distinction.

The theological implication is that Allah takes into account the difference between
ignorance and willful suppression. The former is innocent; the latter is culpable. This is
not a polemical accusation to deploy against interlocutors — it is an internal diagnostic
that explains why intellectual engagement alone cannot reach the person whose barrier is
hawā or gharaḍ. The barrier is not epistemic; it is volitional. See F2 for the
intervention.

**Category C — Cognitive-access limits (not blameworthy)**

The third cause is genuine cognitive limitation — a case where the person does not have
ordinary access to the kind of discursive or recognitional movement the engagement would
normally presuppose.

People in this category are not blameworthy. The tradition's account of divine justice —
calibrated to what a person actually has access to (the principle of ḥujjah) — applies
here. Compassion is the appropriate disposition; intellectual pressure is not.

**Diagnostic use:** Do not use Category C as a first-line label from ordinary discourse.
Only let it govern routing when there is direct evidence of cognitive inaccessibility, or
after sustained engagement has exhausted the matched instruments without yielding any live
differentiator. In the existing corpus the operational consequence is P1 Stage 7: suspend
active discursive pressure without turning that suspension into a verdict on the person's
state before God.

## Deformation Code Emission

The diagnostic terminates in one of the eight short codes used throughout the skill; no
re-abbreviation. Compound cases emit a pair; the primary is named first, the secondary
second, in the order of required intervention (outside in).

| Surface / inferential cue | Deformation code |
|---------------------------|------------------|
| Filter treated as neutral common sense; evidence narrowed silently before any claim is examined | `i'tiqadat-mawrutha` |
| One foundational premise regenerates the downstream conclusions every time local objections are answered | `mushabara-fasida` (subsection of `i'tiqadat-mawrutha`) |
| Objections accelerate once one is dissolved; affective intensity disproportionate to the argument | `hawa` |
| Position held with the texture of a conclusion but never examined; absorbed from milieu | `zann` |
| Acquired by imitation; no live taḥqīq; applies symmetrically to belief and unbelief | `taqlid` |
| Distorted framework felt as direct experience; āyāt pass without resonance | `ada` |
| Honest inquiry feels costly in a specific external stake | `gharad` |
| The barrier genuinely yields to intellectual clearing; no covert volitional or framework layer | `shubha` |

Compound emission uses the pipe: `hawa | zann`, `i'tiqadat-mawrutha | shubha`, etc. The
Compound Case section above fixes the sequencing: ʿāda before iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha, gharaḍ
and hawā before any intellectual content, iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha before evidence can land,
shubha (if genuine) last. The emission mirrors that order.

When the barrier presented as shubha is covering one of the other six (the default
suspicion that §7 above specifies), emit the covering deformation first and the shubha
second only if the intellectual surface still has live content once the cover is lifted:
`hawa | shubha` is legitimate; `shubha | hawa` is usually a misread.

**Posture markers that pull toward specific emissions.** These are not separate
deformations; they are surface-markers that help disambiguate compound cases. Each
routes the primary emission:

- *Mukābara* — obstinate denial of what the subject has already recognized as true —
  pulls toward `hawa` or `gharad` in the deformation axis, and toward `istikbar` or
  `inkar` in the concealment axis. The diagnostic is: the subject is not disputing
  whether the claim is true but refusing to be moved by it.
- *Muʿānada* — contentious rejection (argument-for-argument's-sake against what would
  otherwise be granted) — pulls toward `hawa` on the deformation axis and `juhud` or
  `istikbar` on the concealment axis. The diagnostic is: the subject's objection-
  generation outpaces the arguments being offered, with no intent to converge.
- *Tawāṭuʾ* — collusive or schooled agreement (holding a position because one's
  school or intellectual community all hold it) — pulls toward `taqlid`, and when it
  protects a single framework-level presupposition, toward `mushabara-fasida` as
  compound. Apply V7 symmetrically.
- *Safsaṭa* (sophistic drift) — skepticism about first principles generated as a
  downstream consequence of pressing naturalism or evidentialism consistently — pulls
  toward `i'tiqadat-mawrutha | mushabara-fasida` and calls V9 (necessary-knowledge
  priority) as the matched instrument.

# Modes of Concealment — Full Treatment

## Preamble

The modes of concealment describe how recognition — already present in the fiṭrah
(the primordial cognitive disposition toward truth) — is being withheld from expression.
They are not the same as the seven deformations: the deformations describe what has
suppressed the fiṭrah's operation; the modes describe the relationship between the
subject's inner state and their outer expression.

The modes matter because they determine what kind of engagement is appropriate. The mode
of concealment governs the register; the deformation governs the specific instrument.

The taxonomy is not closed at any fixed count. Earlier drafts spoke of "four modes"; the
current treatment distinguishes five (`irad`, `juhud`, `inkar`, `istikbar`, `nifaq`),
and further refinement is possible wherever a phenomenologically distinct mode demands
a distinct register. The heading is "Modes of Concealment" rather than a count so the
architecture is not frozen by numeric reification.

### Lexical vs. sharʿī note

These modes are used diagnostically, not as imputations of the full sharʿī category of
*kufr* (theological unbelief standing over against *īmān*). The Arabic root *k-f-r*
carries a generalized lexical sense — *covering*, refusing, concealing, suppressing,
denying truth or fact — and that lexical field is useful for noetic diagnosis. The
skill uses this root-sense analytically when naming how recognition is covered or
withheld. This analytic use does not, on its own, constitute the theological judgment
that a specific person stands in the relation of *kufr* to *īmān* in the sharʿī sense.
That theological judgment has its own conditions, which this diagnostic does not
adjudicate. See `references/terminology.md` for the full treatment of this distinction.

---

## Iʿrāḍ — Turning Away

**Character:** Refusal to attend; decline to investigate when the opportunity is
available; withholding of attention from the question itself. The subject is not
rejecting a specific answer and not yet repudiating the truth under recognition; they
are declining to look — turning away before the matter has been allowed to press.
Iʿrāḍ is primarily an orientation of attention, not of assent.

**Phenomenology:** The subject does not engage the question seriously enough to deny
or affirm anything substantive. They change the subject, mark the matter as "not for
them," invoke busyness or lack of interest, reframe the question as somebody else's
concern, or simply disengage. There is no strong repudiative energy; there is a quiet
aversion to examination. The question is not being fought — it is being left un-looked-at.

**Epistemic status:** The fiṭrah's recognition may be present but is not being allowed
a hearing. The subject has not formed the repudiation that juḥūd involves; they have
declined the inquiry that would have made the matter live.

**What works:** Do not dump argument; the subject has not contracted for inquiry. Name
the aversion directly without accusation: "You seem not so much to disagree as to not
want to look at this. What would make it worth looking at?" Offer an invitation to
investigate on terms the subject can actually accept. Character-as-evidence applies —
the quality of presence can make inquiry feel safe where argument cannot. Leaving one
honest question alive is often the correct move.

**What does not work:** Piling on more arguments; moralizing the aversion; treating the
non-engagement as if it were a considered position to be refuted. Each of these hardens
the turning-away into something more defined.

**Distinguishing from `juhud`:** Juhūd is a stronger repudiative posture — the subject
has come close enough to the matter to deny it. Iʿrāḍ is the prior condition: the
subject has not yet let the matter come close. Many cases that look like juhūd on the
surface are really iʿrāḍ underneath, and the register needed is invitational rather
than confrontational.

**Worldview-deflection note:** A slogan such as "I have no religion, I just follow the
evidence wherever it leads" can instantiate `irad` when pseudo-neutrality is functioning
as deflection from letting the matter press. The criterion burden is still diagnosed in
full; what changes is the directly deployable register, not the existence of the diagnosis.

Do not flatten this family into an abstract concealment label alone. Several distinct reads
must stay visible at once:

- **Sincere but inherited pseudo-neutrality:** the speaker may genuinely think they are
  describing openness while carrying an inherited tribunal as invisible common sense
- **Active pre-inquiry deflection:** the slogan is being used to keep the question from
  pressing or to control register before inquiry has actually begun
- **Criterion-smuggling as invisible filter:** the pseudo-neutral claim is itself how the
  foreign premise enters the case, even when no concealment mode has yet been confirmed
- **`irad` as interactional posture:** `irad` applies when the slogan is functioning as
  turning-away or register-control, not merely whenever the slogan-family appears

The practical consequence is selective deployment. The system may keep the full internal
diagnosis — pseudo-neutral reason, imported criterion, worldview denial, hidden ʿaqīdah —
while letting the stronger present cue govern the immediate Layer B move. Default sequence:
ask one bounded diagnostic question first; if the same blocker stays live, add only minimal
tribunal-clearing; then pause. Concealment analysis here is therefore a live route into
deployment discipline, not a label that exhausts the case.

---

## Juḥūd — Culpable Denial

**Character:** Refusal of acknowledgment once the matter has been allowed to press —
repudiation of a truth that has, at some level, become present. Juḥūd is not mere
non-engagement; it is the active posture of saying *no* to what would otherwise be
recognized. Juḥūd is primarily volitional and is directed at the matter as something
now sufficiently in view to be denied.

**Phenomenology:** The subject engages the question enough to repudiate it. There is a
quality of declared refusal — a position is being held *against* the recognition.
Endless qualifications, questioning of the premises of questions, or flat declaration
that the matter cannot be so — each of these presupposes that the matter has been
allowed close enough to require pushing back on.

**Epistemic status:** The fiṭrah's recognition is present and has been registered enough
to occasion the denial. In some cases it is precisely the pressure of incipient
recognition that generates the repudiation.

**What works:** Arguments alone do not land; the barrier is not an information gap.
The barrier must be named — gently and directly — not argued past. Character-as-evidence
applies. Sometimes the most honest move is to name what is happening: "This seems less
like confusion than like refusal. What would it take for this not to be refusal?" The
maieutic register (P4) may help if the denial has a seam of inner recognition; otherwise
the work is relational and long.

**What does not work:** More elaborate argument; pressing for agreement; treating the
denial as if it were an intellectual position that could be dislodged by producing a
better proof.

**Distinguishing from `irad`:** Iʿrāḍ turns away before the matter presses; juḥūd
repudiates once it has. The two frequently appear as a sequence: iʿrāḍ is broken by
the matter becoming unavoidable, and the subject then either accepts or shifts into
juḥūd. A case-state that starts in iʿrāḍ and moves to juḥūd is a sign that attention
has successfully been brought to the matter, even if the outcome is denial rather than
acceptance.

---

## Inkār — Rejection

**Character:** Outward denial despite inward recognition already present in the qalb.
There is a gap between what has been registered internally and what is admitted on the
tongue. The subject knows — or at some level recognizes — but will not acknowledge it.

**Phenomenology:** This is the most poignant mode. The subject is often under real
internal pressure — the recognition is present and generating distress, but the
acknowledgment is not available. Sometimes this manifests as intensity of objection
disproportionate to the intellectual force of the point being resisted: the person
is arguing hard against something that has in some sense already landed.

**Distinguishing from juhūd:** Juhūd is flat refusal; inkār is active resistance
against something present. The quality of engagement is different — inkār often has
more energy, more urgency, than juhūd's flatness.

**What works:** The maieutic procedure (`references/procedures/P4-maieutic.md`) — not argument, but
questions that create space for the recognition that is already present to surface.
"What do you actually think when you're not arguing?" "What happens when you're not
defending this position?" Patience. R2 (the Reminder) directed at what is already
present but unacknowledged.

**What does not work:** Intellectual argument that the subject can absorb into their
pattern of resistance.

---

## Istikbār — Obstinacy

**Character:** Acknowledgment without volitional alignment. The subject may actually
concede, intellectually, that a position has force — but the will is not aligned with
the recognition. There is a kind of pride or resistance that is distinct from
intellectual doubt.

**Phenomenology:** The subject can articulate the argument clearly, can even grant it,
but something in them refuses to move. "Yes, I can see why you would say that" combined
with no actual movement. The acknowledgment is genuine but the alignment is absent.

**Distinguishing from gharaḍ:** Gharaḍ has something specific at stake — a cost
to be avoided. Istikbār is more fundamental — a disposition of the will against
submission regardless of cost. It is related to pride (kibr) in the classical sense.

**What works:** Relational and spiritual engagement, not intellectual content. The
resistance is not to an argument but to the act of acknowledgment itself. Building a
genuine relationship in which acknowledgment is not experienced as defeat — in which
submitting to truth is experienced as dignity rather than loss — is the long work.
Character as evidence is central here.

**What does not work:** More arguments. The interlocutor already concedes the
arguments.

---

## Nifāq — Surface Acceptance

**Character:** Outward agreement without genuine inner conviction. The subject performs
acceptance — says the right things, may even act in accordance with them in some
contexts — but the inner reorganization has not occurred. The surface and the depth
are not aligned.

**Epistemic status:** This mode is not dishonesty in the simple sense — often the
subject genuinely wants to hold the position, presents themselves as holding it, but
has not done the work of genuine conviction. The position sits on the surface without
having been genuinely inhabited.

**Phenomenology:** The subject's position collapses quickly under pressure, or shows
inconsistency in contexts where they are not consciously maintaining the presentation.
What they say they believe and how they actually reason do not cohere.

**What works:** Building alignment between surface and depth — not by more argument
but by the cultivation of genuine conviction. The already-believing procedure
(`references/procedures/P5-already-believing.md`) is designed precisely for this: moving from inherited or surface
acceptance to examined, stable conviction. Questions that require the person to
actually inhabit their stated position, not just report it.

**What does not work:** Treating the surface acceptance as if it were genuine conviction
and building on it. The construction will collapse.

---

## On Mixed Modes

Real interlocutors often present multiple modes simultaneously, or shift between them.
The dāʿī should track which mode is operative in a given exchange and be willing to shift
their approach when the mode shifts. An engagement may begin with juhūd, move to inkār
as recognition begins to surface, and require patience with istikbār before alignment
becomes available. Reading the mode accurately is as important as reading the deformation.

---

## Concealment Mode Code Emission

The mode read terminates in a single code that feeds the case-state line. The codes are
the transliterated names used throughout the skill; no re-abbreviation.

| Surface pattern | Mode code | Register governed |
|-----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Turning away, non-engagement, decline to investigate despite opportunity, quiet aversion to examination; subject will not let the matter press | `irad` | Do not dump argument; name the aversion invitationally; leave one honest question live; character-as-evidence; no moralizing |
| Culpable denial once the matter has been allowed to press; repudiative posture; endless qualification, flat declaration, or pushing-back-against an already-registered matter | `juhud` | Intellectual argument will not land; name the barrier (not argue past it); character-as-evidence; relational work; maieutic (P4) if a seam of inner recognition is visible |
| Active resistance disproportionate to the argument; pressure of unacknowledged recognition | `inkar` | Maieutic (P4); R2 Reminder; do not press argument the subject can absorb |
| Intellectual concession without volitional movement; pride-structure visible | `istikbar` | Relational/spiritual register; suspend further argument; character-as-evidence central |
| Performative agreement without inner reorganization; collapses under pressure | `nifaq` | Already-believing procedure (P5); questions requiring the subject to inhabit the stated position |
| Two modes genuinely co-present | `<mode-A> | <mode-B>` | Name the register each mode requires; do not force collapse |
| Mode still unreadable after genuine attention | `mode-?` | Route through `mixed-case-handling.md` before committing to register |

**Boundary note — `irad` vs. `juhud`:** The two most frequently confused modes. `irad`
is attention not yet given; `juhud` is acknowledgment refused once attention has landed.
The register required is almost opposite: `irad` wants invitation and room, `juhud` wants
the barrier named. Misrouting `irad` as `juhud` hardens the turning-away into something
more defined; misrouting `juhud` as `irad` keeps offering invitations that the subject
has already moved past refusing. When uncertain, read whether the matter has been
allowed to press. If not, the mode is `irad` regardless of the surface vigor of the
refusal.

The concealment mode constrains the register — it answers *what kind of engagement can
land at all*. It is distinct from the seven deformations (which answer *what has suppressed
the fiṭrah's operation*) and from DO-orient (which answers *what the discourse is for*).
All three are needed before a case-library module can be loaded with confidence.

## Minimal-Pair Discriminators

### Minimal-Pair Discriminator: Iʿrāḍ vs. Juḥūd

*Decision rule:* Has the matter been allowed to press? Iʿrāḍ is prior to engagement; juḥūd is refusal after engagement has occurred.

*Left correct (iʿrāḍ):* An acquaintance changes the subject when a religious question is mentioned in passing. No argument has been presented; no position has engaged theirs. The mode is iʿrāḍ — attention has not yet been given. Correct response: invitational register, one question left live, no intellectual content deployed. Incorrect if diagnosed as juḥūd: naming a barrier that hasn't yet formed hardens iʿrāḍ into something more defined.

*Right correct (juḥūd):* The same person, in a later conversation, explicitly acknowledges having read an argument, grants its internal logic, but dismisses it as "not for them" while showing clear signs of repudiation. Engagement has occurred; the refusal is post-engagement. The mode is juḥūd. Correct response: name the barrier. Incorrect if still diagnosed as iʿrāḍ: continuing invitational mode fails to address the now-operative repudiation.

*Differentiating signal:* Has the interlocutor demonstrated engagement with the substance — even if to dismiss it? If yes, juḥūd is in play. If the substance has never been engaged, iʿrāḍ governs.

---

### Minimal-Pair Discriminator: Juḥūd vs. Inkār

*Decision rule:* Is there evidence of inner recognition being suppressed, or is the refusal an outward stance with no detectable inner recognition?

*Left correct (juḥūd):* Interlocutor responds to a fiṭrah-directed sign by intellectually dismissing it ("that's just psychology") without any affect suggesting inner movement. The refusal is repudiative but shows no seam of recognition. Mode is juḥūd. Correct response: relational register, barrier named; not P4.

*Right correct (inkār):* Interlocutor responds to the same sign with visible affect, attempts to change the subject immediately, or gives an unusually vehement dismissal inconsistent with intellectual confidence. The vigor of the denial exceeds what intellectual disagreement requires. Mode is inkār — recognition appears to be present and is being actively resisted. Correct response: P4 (maieutic); not more argument.

*Differentiating signal:* Disproportionate vigor of denial, subject-change after a sign lands, or affective charge inconsistent with the stated intellectual confidence. Any of these suggests inkār over juḥūd.

---

## Revelation-Transmission (RT) Axis — When to Emit

The concealment diagnostic also sits adjacent to the revelation-transmission axis. When
the live objection is about scripture, transmission, canon, or prophetic claim, an RT code
is additionally emitted from the routing at `case-library/INDEX.md`:

| Live pressure | RT code |
|---------------|---------|
| Viral manuscript, fragment, or citation asserted to overturn authenticated transmission | `RT-1` |
| Canon-formation framed as destabilizing revelation-status | `RT-2` |
| Qirāʾāt / aḥruf / manuscript plurality framed as corruption | `RT-3` |
| Believer destabilized by text-history pressure (internal crisis, not debate) | `RT-4` |

routing to `case-library/revelation-transmission.md`.

# Discourse Orientation — Full Treatment

## Preamble

The most consequential diagnostic question is often not which deformation is present or
which mode of concealment is operative — but what the discourse is actually ordered toward.
Intellectual content directed at an engagement that is not genuinely truth-seeking passes
beneath the level at which the engagement is operating.

This diagnostic runs before selecting any intellectual response. It determines whether
intellectual engagement is the right instrument at all.

---

## Toward Truth and Warrant

**Character:** The subject is genuinely attempting to determine what is real. Disagreement
is sincere. Objections are offered because the subject genuinely cannot see past them.
Movement toward a conclusion would be accepted if it were earned.

**Distinguishing marks:** The discourse converges — not necessarily toward agreement, but
toward greater precision about where exactly the disagreement lies. Questions are asked
because answers are wanted. The subject engages with the responses given, not merely
uses them as occasions for new objections.

**What follows:** The full apparatus of the skill applies. Intellectual engagement is
appropriate and productive. The deformation and mode-of-concealment diagnostics determine
which instrument is needed; the discourse orientation diagnostic confirms that intellectual
engagement is the right register.

---

## Identity-Performance

**Character:** The position is being held and expressed primarily as a marker of social
belonging, group membership, or self-concept. The intellectual content of the position
is secondary to the act of holding and displaying it. The position functions as a
credential, an affiliation signal, or a component of self-presentation.

**Distinguishing from gharaḍ:** Gharaḍ involves something external at stake — a cost
to be avoided. Identity-performance is constitutive — the position is part of who the
subject presents themselves as being. Holding the position differently would mean being
a different kind of person.

**Distinguishing marks:** The subject engages with positions at the level of what they
*signal* rather than what they *establish*. Conceding a point feels like a betrayal of
identity. Moving toward the interlocutor's position feels like defection from a community.
The energy of the engagement is social and reputational, not epistemological.

**What follows:** Intellectual engagement with the position's content will be consumed
but will not move the subject — because movement would mean ceasing to perform the
identity. The productive response is to name the distinction: "there is a difference
between holding a position because you have examined it and holding it because of what
it represents — which is this?" Do not supply more content to be performed against.
Relational engagement and the longer work of making honest inquiry available are the
appropriate instruments. See P7 Stop 1 (Content-Withholding Stop) in
`references/procedures/P7-restoration-stops.md` — identity-performance is a named
trigger for that stop.

---

## Novelty-Seeking / Autotelic Stimulation

**Character:** Ideas are being engaged for the pleasure of engagement itself — for
intellectual stimulation, the enjoyment of complexity, the experience of inhabiting an
interesting or unusual position. The question of truth is not being seriously inhabited.
The discourse is an end in itself.

**Distinguishing marks:** The subject moves freely between positions, showing more
interest in the elegance or novelty of a view than in whether it is true. Intellectual
content is consumed with apparent enthusiasm but produces no movement toward commitment.
The conversation feels generative but arrives nowhere. When pressed toward a conclusion,
the subject retreats into complexity or introduces a new angle.

**What follows:** Do not mistake this for genuine shubhah. A genuine shubhah, once
dissolved, releases the recognition it was blocking. Autotelic engagement does not
resolve — it self-reproduces. The productive response is to name what is happening
directly: "you are engaging this as an intellectual exercise rather than as a genuine
question about what is real." Ask whether genuine inquiry is the goal. If genuine inquiry
is disclaimed, no further intellectual engagement is owed on this occasion. The fiṭrah
is present; the discourse is not oriented toward it. Leave a question alive and present,
rather than pressing. The fiṭrah works in the intervals.

---

## Conjecture Without Anchor (Ẓann as Mode)

**Character:** Positions are asserted without discipline of warrant. The subject moves
freely between claims without tracking logical consistency, evidential weight, or the
relations among their premises. The discourse generates assertions and counter-assertions
at a rate that outpaces genuine examination.

**Distinguishing from ẓann as deformation:** Ẓann as deformation is about specific
assumed-by-default conclusions treated as examined. Ẓann as mode of discourse is a
pervasive pattern — the subject operates consistently without grounding their claims.

**Distinguishing marks:** Premises and conclusions are not tracked; the subject does not
notice or care when they contradict themselves; each new assertion is launched without
examining how it relates to previous ones. The discourse is wide but shallow.

**What follows:** Do not engage the general position. Press for the specific grounds of
one specific claim at a time. Do not allow movement to the next claim until the current
one can be examined and held stable. "Before we go on — what exactly are you claiming
here, and what are you basing it on?" The goal is not to win a point but to introduce
the discipline of warrant into the exchange. Once the subject is engaging at the level
of specific, grounded claims, the other instruments become available.

---

## Mixed or Unstable Orientation

Most real engagements combine modes. Genuine curiosity alongside identity-performance.
Real intellectual difficulty alongside autotelic stimulation. A person may begin an
exchange in conjecture mode and move toward genuine inquiry once something lands.

The practitioner should:
- Identify the predominant mode and respond to it
- Remain alert to shifts in orientation, especially shifts toward genuine inquiry —
  these are the moments when the full apparatus becomes available
- Not assume that a mixed orientation is stable — the truth-seeking element, however
  small, is what the engagement is in service of
- Leave space for orientation to shift without pressing for it

---

## DO-Orient Code Emission

The orientation read terminates in a short code (`DO-orient`) that routes downstream. It
is not the same as the doctrinal DO-1..15 codes in `case-library/INDEX.md`; it rides
beside them. Both are named with the "DO" prefix because both belong to the discourse
axis: DO-orient answers *what the discourse is for*; DO-1..15 answers *what objection
family is live inside that discourse*.

| Orientation read | DO-orient code | What it governs |
|------------------|----------------|------------------|
| Toward truth and warrant | `truth-seek` | Full intellectual apparatus applies; route via NS and doctrinal DO codes |
| Identity-performance | `identity-perf` | Withhold more content; name the distinction; shift to relational register |
| Novelty-seeking / autotelic stimulation | `autotelic` | Do not mistake for shubhah; leave one question alive; stop feeding stimulation |
| Conjecture without anchor (ẓann as mode) | `zann-mode` | Suspend movement; press for grounds of one claim at a time before any larger move |
| Mixed / unstable | `mixed` | Name the predominant orientation and note the minority channel; stay alert for shift |

If the orientation is `identity-perf`, `autotelic`, or `zann-mode`, the correct next step
is almost never a case-library entry — even if the NS code is clear. The doctrinal module
is the right content at the wrong register. Name the orientation first; return to content
once the register is truth-seeking, or once a question has been left alive long enough for
the fiṭrah to do its work.

---

## Doctrinal DO-1..15 Routing Note

The DO-1..15 codes in `case-library/INDEX.md` live on a different axis. They are emitted
from the NS + problem-family diagnostic, not from this file. When scripture-history pressure
is live the corresponding axis is RT-1..4. This file's job is the orientation read; other
files emit the doctrinal and transmission codes. All three axes — NS code, DO-orient, and
(where applicable) doctrinal DO / RT — appear in the final case-state line.

---

## Output Shape

The orientation diagnostic terminates with one line feeding `case-state-schema.md`:

    DO-orient: <truth-seek | identity-perf | autotelic | zann-mode | mixed>    Next: <smallest matched move>

*Engagement posture* is an internal diagnostic distinction — not a separately emitted IR
field. It names whether intellectual engagement is apt at all (intellectual / relational /
suspended), and that judgment is folded into the downstream `Register` selection emitted
by the concealment-mode diagnostic via `case-state-schema.md`. Concretely: a DO-orient of
`identity-perf`, `autotelic`, or `zann-mode` forces the emitted `Register` into
`relational` or `suspended` regardless of the concealment-mode read; a DO-orient of
`truth-seek` allows the concealment-mode read to select among
`invitational | intellectual | maieutic | relational | suspended` on its own terms. The
posture governs the register; the register is what appears on the wire.

If the orientation is `truth-seek`, the noetic-reading-checklist's NS emission plus the
concealment-mode emission plus (where applicable) the doctrinal DO or RT code compose the
full case-state line. If the orientation is anything else, the DO-orient line governs and
the other emissions remain available but not acted on until the register shifts.

---

## Minimal-Pair Discriminator: Gharaḍ (Vested Interest) vs. Identity-Performance

*Decision rule:* Gharaḍ is a deformation — something is at stake that makes honest inquiry threatening. Identity-performance is a discourse orientation — the position is being held and expressed as a credential or affiliation marker. They can co-occur but require different instruments.

*Left correct (gharaḍ):* Interlocutor is a scientist who has publicly and repeatedly stated their atheism. The vested interest is concrete: professional identity, published positions, and social capital are at stake. The deformation is gharaḍ — the cost of honest inquiry is visible and real. The discourse orientation may still be truth-seeking at surface level. Correct instrument: F2 (name the cost); not identity-performance mode.

*Right correct (identity-performance):* Interlocutor is a university student who signals their atheism prominently, has no professional stake, but treats every exchange as an opportunity to perform their identity before implicit social witnesses. Every intellectual point is absorbed into self-presentation. The orientation is identity-performance; gharaḍ is minimal. Correct instrument: name the register, defer content; not F2 (there is no specific cost to name).

*Differentiating signal:* Is there a concrete external stake — professional, social, financial, relational — that would be materially threatened by inquiry? If yes, gharaḍ is operative. If the position functions primarily as self-presentation without a concrete external stake, identity-performance governs.

## Coverage Verification

- Failure condition: Do not select an intellectual content module before establishing whether the discourse is truth-seeking, identity-performance, autotelic, zann-mode, or mixed.
- IR-visible consequence: Emit `DO-orient` and the resulting register/hold implication; non-truth-seeking orientations hold doctrinal content even when the NS or DO family is otherwise clear.
- Minimal pair: Gharaḍ is a deformation with a concrete stake; identity-performance is an orientation where the position functions as self-presentation.
- Hold/release rule: Hold doctrinal modules while identity-performance, autotelic, or zann-mode governs; release content only after orientation shifts toward truth-seeking or a bounded question becomes the permissible move.
- Anti-pattern guard: Do not misread lively engagement as inquiry when the exchange is being consumed for identity, novelty, or unanchored assertion.

# M5 — Deformation Triage

## Role of M5 in V1

M5 is the deformation-sorting subroutine called inside V1 Phase 3 (triage). It does not
replace V1 as the entry gate. The canonical compound-case sequence lives in
`references/diagnostics/seven-deformations.md` §The Compound Case; M5 applies it.

## The Triage Sequence

1. Confirm the NS code from `references/diagnostics/noetic-reading-checklist.md`. The NS
   profile already implies a likely primary deformation — treat it as a prior, not as a
   conclusion.
2. Ask whether the upstream block is a general inherited *framework* (iʿtiqādāt
   mawrūtha), a habituated *pattern* (ʿāda), or a single *faulty presupposition*
   (mushābara fāsida) anchoring specific conclusions. The three are addressed differently
   and cannot be swapped.
3. Read the deformation axis per `references/diagnostics/seven-deformations.md`. Emit a
   primary deformation code or a compound pair in sequencing order (outside in).
4. Cross-check the concealment mode
   (`references/diagnostics/modes-of-concealment.md`) and DO-orient
   (`references/diagnostics/discourse-orientation.md`). If either axis is still
   under-signalled, hand the case back to V1 rather than forcing a tactic choice.
5. Select the smallest matched instrument.

are co-present, consult the row matching the primary and the column matching the
secondary. The cell names the first move and the transition condition.

sequencing rule (outside in) determines which deformation is "primary" for the triage,
which is usually the one whose presence would make the other's intervention misfire.

## Concealment × Deformation Cross-Check

The deformation answers *what has suppressed the fiṭrah*; the concealment answers *what
the subject's inner/outer relation is*. The two together determine whether intellectual
content is the right register at all:

| Concealment | Deformation pairing | Register implication |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| `irad` | Any | Invitational register; do not dump argument; name the aversion; leave one honest question live; matched instrument waits on attention being given |
| `juhud` | Any | Argument will not land; character-as-evidence; name the barrier (do not argue past it); maieutic (P4) if a seam of inner recognition is visible |
| `inkar` | With hawa / gharad | Maieutic (P4) + R2; do not feed absorbable argument |
| `istikbar` | With hawa | Relational/spiritual; pride-structure is the barrier, not content |
| `nifaq` | With taqlid or zann | Already-believing procedure (P5); questions that require inhabited belief |
| `mode-?` | Any | Hand back to V1; do not commit a tactic |

If concealment is any of the first five (`irad`, `juhud`, `inkar`, `istikbar`, `nifaq`)
and the case is not `truth-seek` in DO-orient, the matched deformation instrument waits
on the register shift. M5 does not overrule the orientation read — it composes with it.

**`irad` vs. `juhud` boundary at M5:** The deformation triage is the same for both
(ʿāda/hawā still need F2, etc.), but the *register* in which the matched instrument is
delivered differs sharply. Under `irad`, the instrument is held until the subject has
let the matter press; under `juhud`, the instrument is delivered in a relational frame
that does not argue past the refusal. Misrouting between the two results in either
pressure on a non-engaged subject (argument becomes intrusion) or endless invitation to
a subject who has already moved past needing it.

## Critical Warning

Presenting intellectual content to someone whose barrier is vested interest, entrenched
will, identity-performance, or ẓann-mode discourse does not merely fail — it provides
new material for resistance or stimulation to organize around and actively hardens the
barrier. The most common failure mode in sustained epistemological engagement is
failing to triage.

## Router Guardrails

M5 sorts deformation; it does not overrule family routing once the live case-type is
clear.
- If the presenting pressure is manuscript, canon, preservation, original-text, or
  text-history destabilization, route through V10 and the matched RT case before M5
  becomes the opening architecture.
- If the pressure is Trinity, incarnation coherence, perfect-being-to-Trinity, or
  philosopher's-God, route through V8 and the matched DO case after deformation
  sorting.
- If the pressure is a discursive attack on necessary knowledge itself, route to V9
rather than treating the issue as generic shubhah management.

## Category C Caution

The Category C note in `references/diagnostics/seven-deformations.md` is not a
first-line routing label for surface discourse. It governs suspension of pressure only
after direct evidence of cognitive inaccessibility, or after sustained engagement has
exhausted every matched instrument without a live differentiator. Never use Category C
to exit a difficult case; use it only to refuse a verdict on a person whose access is
genuinely constrained.

## Output Shape

M5 terminates with:

    Deformation: <primary> [| <secondary>]    First instrument: <matched>    Sequencing note (if compound): <what must clear before the second>    Register-hold: <none | pending-DO-orient-shift | pending-concealment-shift>

The register-hold field tells V1 whether the instrument is immediately deployable or
waits on a register shift the concealment or DO-orient axis has already flagged.
