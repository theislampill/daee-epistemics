# Arabic-Backbone Predicates — Operational Checks from the Tradition

These predicates distill the tradition's core epistemic commitments into typed checks. They are not theses to be argued; they are conditions to be checked. Each predicate either fires (is true of the case) or does not. When a predicate fires, it specifies a route consequence. The practitioner either marks it `true`, `false`, or `unknown` — there is no option of elegant prose that bypasses the check.

The predicates are organized into four groups: criterion predicates, tribunal predicates, ordering predicates, and category predicates. Each group addresses a distinct structural failure mode.

---

## Group 1 — Criterion Predicates

These check whether a foreign criterion is functioning as the measure to which the religious position must answer.

---

### Predicate C-1: Smuggled Criterion

**Check:** Has a criterion been imported as neutral reason when it is not?

**Operational form:** "The standard being applied to this claim — [state it explicitly] — was not derived from neutral rational necessity. It was inherited from a specific philosophical tradition [name it] and is being applied as if it were self-evidently the correct measure for all claims."

**True when:** The interlocutor applies an epistemological standard (empirical verifiability, inferential necessity, historical-critical neutrality) to religious claims that they do not apply with the same rigor to their own foundational commitments — or that cannot certify itself by its own standard.

**Route consequence (true):** V2 mandatory before content. The criterion is named, shown to be one position among alternatives, and shown to be non-self-grounding. Content engagement is blocked until C-1 clears.

**Route consequence (false or not applicable):** Proceed to the next predicate group.

**Prohibited move when true:** Accepting the criterion as the legitimate bar and attempting to satisfy it. This grants the chair.

---

### Predicate C-2: Consistent Application Check

**Check:** Is the criterion being applied to religious claims that the interlocutor is not applying to their own foundational commitments of equivalent epistemic status?

**Operational form:** "Apply your criterion here: [state it]. Now apply it to your commitment to [other minds / the external world / the uniformity of nature / the reliability of memory / the objectivity of moral facts]. Does it pass the same test?"

**True when:** The criterion produces rejection of theistic belief while passing commitments of equivalent epistemic fragility.

**Route consequence (true):** M1 (self-refutation) applied to the criterion itself, not to the first-order argument. The criterion is shown to be inconsistently applied — which does not establish theism but dissolves the criterion's authority as a gate.

---

### Predicate C-3: Criterion Self-Grounding Check

**Check:** Can the criterion establish its own truth by the standards it recommends?

**Operational form:** "Empiricism cannot be established empirically. The principle that only necessary truths are knowledge is not itself a necessary truth. Historical-critical neutrality cannot be established by historical-critical method without circularity. Does your criterion pass its own test?"

**True when:** The criterion cannot certify itself — which is the case for virtually all global epistemological constraints.

**Route consequence (true):** V3 (regress dissolution) applied to the criterion's own justification. The criterion collapses to a basic commitment — which means other basic commitments are in play.

---

## Group 2 — Tribunal Predicates

These check whether a foreign authority structure is operating upstream as the legitimate judge to which the tradition must answer.

---

### Predicate T-1: Counterfeit Tribunal

**Check:** Is a philosophical or academic system currently functioning as the upstream authority that revelation must satisfy before it is accepted?

**Operational form:** "What would it take for this claim [state the religious claim] to be accepted as legitimate? Who is the judge? What kind of response would satisfy them?"

**True when:** The answer to "who is the judge?" is a philosophical tradition (Aristotelian metaphysics, secular historical criticism, scientific naturalism, liberal political philosophy) rather than the tradition's own epistemic criteria (tawātur, ḍarūrī recognition, the fiṭrah's deliverances under sound conditions).

**Route consequence (true):** Name the tribunal explicitly. The tribunal has not been appointed by any legitimate authority — it has been installed by default or by the interlocutor's intellectual formation. The first move is not to satisfy the tribunal's requirements but to question its jurisdiction. Foreign-premise-detection.md Step 2 (source tradition) and Step 3 (functional role) govern.

**Prohibited move when true:** Producing a response that treats the tribunal's requirements as the legitimate question and attempts to satisfy them. This ratifies the tribunal's authority even while contesting its verdict.

---

### Predicate T-2: Prophetic Discourse Subjected to Kalām Categories

**Check:** Is the response treating prophetic discourse as if it were accountable to later kalāmic or philosophical categories — as if the prophets should have opened by satisfying the kalāmic demonstration requirement, or as if waḥy must be validated by post-prophetic rational theology before it carries weight?

**Operational form:** "The prophets did not open their missions by constructing or requiring a discursive kalāmic proof. They directed attention to āyāt (signs). The kalāmic apparatus developed later as a remedial route for impaired recognition — it is not the universal first path of prophetic address. Is the current response treating it as if it were?"

**True when:** The response has accepted the frame that revelation must first clear a rationalist or kalāmic bar before it can be presented as a knowledge source — rather than presenting revelation as the primary source and kalāmic argument as a secondary clarifying or restorative tool.

**Route consequence (true):** Reframe. Restore the correct order: prophetic address proceeds through signs, recognition, and the fiṭrah's response. Kalāmic demonstration is remedial and secondary — not a gatekeeper for revelation's legitimacy.

---

### Predicate T-3: Later Foundation Mistaken for Pure Foundation

**Check:** Has a later technical philosophical or theological foundation been presented (by the interlocutor or accepted by the response) as if it were the religion's own pure foundation?

**Operational form:** "The mutakallimūn developed their apparatus within specific historical controversies, using categories partly inherited from Aristotelian and neo-Platonic philosophy. These categories are not the Religion's own primary epistemic foundations — they are post-prophetic intellectual instruments, some more faithful to the prophetic sources than others. Is the current engagement treating them as if they were the pure primary foundation?"

**True when:** The kalāmic wujūb al-naẓar, the Aristotelian attribute-simplicity framework, or the neo-Platonic immutability doctrine is being presented as if it were the religion's native starting point — rather than as a later historical development that requires its own examination.

**Route consequence (true):** Distinguish the Religion's foundations (the Qurʾān, the Sunnah, the fiṭrah, the tawātur of the community) from later philosophical frameworks layered on top of them. The later frameworks may be useful, partially faithful, or in tension with the foundations — each case requires examination. The examination begins by naming the distinction.

---

## Group 3 — Ordering Predicates

These check whether the correct epistemic order — the ordering of sources, faculties, and routes to knowledge — is being maintained.

---

### Predicate O-1: Reason-Over-Transmission Inversion

**Check:** Is reason being treated as the root and revelation as the branch — such that revelation's content must be validated by prior rational demonstration before it carries weight?

**True when:** The interlocutor (or the response accepting their frame) holds that revelation may only be credited insofar as reason has already established its content to be plausible. Revelation becomes confirmatory of what reason independently establishes, rather than an independent and primary knowledge source.

**Route consequence (true):** P3 (reason-revelation tension): The tradition's own position is that sound reason and authentic revelation never genuinely conflict, because both issue from the same source. The apparent conflict is located in contaminated reason, weak transmission, or a legitimate tension resolvable by taʿāruḍ principles — not in a genuine priority of reason over revelation. Restore the order: both are sources; neither overrides the other; apparent conflict is located and resolved, not dissolved by subordinating one to the other.

---

### Predicate O-2: Fiṭrī Recognition Demoted Below Its Epistemic Grade

**Check:** Is the fiṭrah's deliverance of theistic recognition being treated as ẓannī (probable, insufficient for knowledge) rather than ḍarūrī (non-inferential, sufficient for knowledge when the fiṭrah is functioning soundly)?

**True when:** The response has accepted a framing in which fiṭrī recognition requires inferential supplement before it counts as knowledge — or has treated it as a mere feeling, inclination, or cultural reflex rather than as a genuine epistemic faculty delivering ḍarūrī knowledge.

**Route consequence (true):** V9 (necessary-knowledge priority): the fiṭrah's delivery is ḍarūrī precisely because it is prior to inference. Inferential argument is a remedial or confirmatory route — legitimate and knowledge-conferring — but not the gatekeeper that licenses recognition to count as knowledge. Name the demotion and reverse it.

---

### Predicate O-3: Sign Converted to Premise

**Check:** Has a sign (āyah) — presented to direct attention and activate fiṭrī recognition — been converted into a logical premise in an argument?

**True when:** The practitioner or interlocutor has taken a sign (the order of the cosmos, a moral intuition, a moment of beauty or awe) and placed it as a premise in a deductive or inductive argument, rather than presenting it as an āyah for the fiṭrah to respond to.

**Route consequence (true):** V5 (directing attention to signs): present the sign, not the argument from the sign. The practitioner who explains the logical implication has converted the sign into a premise and bypassed the recognition mechanism that the sign was meant to activate. Return to sign-presentation; let the fiṭrah make the connection.

---

## Group 4 — Category Predicates

These check for category mistakes that generate spurious apparent conflicts.

---

### Predicate K-1: Unlike Transmission Systems Conflated

**Check:** Are the transmission systems of two different traditions being treated as equivalent in kind — such that a methodological critique developed for one is applied to the other without examining whether the conditions transfer?

**True when:** Historical-critical methods developed for Biblical transmission (single-strand manuscript tradition, disputed canon, no living recitational community) are applied to Qurʾānic transmission (tawātur, living recitational tradition, isnād system) as if the transmission structures were equivalent. Or: the reverse — the tawātur standard is applied to Biblical transmission as if it had the same structure.

**Route consequence (true):** V10 Branch selection (tradition-specific branch operators). Name the specific transmission structure of each tradition. Apply methodological critique only where the conditions of the critique actually obtain. The transmission architecture difference is not a defensive move — it is a factual distinction that changes the epistemic assessment.

---

### Predicate K-2: Lexical and Sharʿī Levels Collapsed

**Check:** Has a term being used in its lexical-analytic sense been received (or deployed) as if it were a full sharʿī judgment?

**True when:** The deformation-emission of `juhūd` (culpable denial in the lexical-analytic sense: the concealment mode is active) is received as a pronouncement of *kufr* in the full sharʿī sense. Or: the use of `gharaḍ` as a diagnostic code is received as an accusation of dishonesty. The sharʿī/lexical distinction is operative throughout the skill — collapse in either direction is an error.

**Route consequence (true):** Explicitly distinguish the level of the claim: "I am naming a cognitive-moral mechanism — how the recognition is being covered — not adjudicating the sharʿī category that requires separate conditions." See `references/terminology.md` for the full treatment.

## Predicate Emission Format

Each predicate that fires contributes to the case-state. The emission format:

```text
[Backbone Predicates]
- C-1 (smuggled criterion): true / false / unknown
- C-2 (consistent application): true / false / unknown
- C-3 (self-grounding): true / false / unknown
- T-1 (counterfeit tribunal): true / false / unknown
- T-2 (prophetic discourse subjected to kalām): true / false / unknown
- T-3 (later foundation mistaken for pure foundation): true / false / unknown
- O-1 (reason-over-transmission inversion): true / false / unknown
- O-2 (fiṭrī recognition demoted): true / false / unknown
- O-3 (sign converted to premise): true / false / unknown
- K-1 (unlike transmission systems conflated): true / false / unknown
- K-2 (lexical/sharʿī collapsed): true / false / unknown
- Active predicates: [list true ones]
- Route consequences: [list what each active predicate requires]
```

**Compression rule:** Do not populate all predicates performatively. Run only the checks relevant to the live case. If no backbone predicate fires, emit `[Backbone Predicates] — none active` and proceed. The purpose is disciplined checking, not exhaustive annotation.

## Trigger Mapping — Minimum Checks by NS Code and DO Family

The following predicates should be checked as a minimum for the named cases. Other predicates may also be active; these are the ones that most commonly fire and are most costly to miss.

| Case surface | Check minimally |
|---|---|
| NS-1 (Naturalist) | C-1, C-2, C-3 (the naturalist framework is almost always C-1 or C-3) |
| NS-6 (Kalāmic Evidentialist) | T-2 (wujūb al-naẓar functions as T-2), T-3 (kalāmic apparatus as "pure foundation") |
| NS-9 (Historical-Critical Skeptic) | K-1 (conflation of transmission systems), C-1 (historical-critical neutrality as smuggled criterion) |
| NS-10 (Māturīdī Evidentialist) | T-2, O-2 (naẓar required before fiṭrī recognition counts as knowledge) |
| DO-5 (Transcendence and language) | O-1, K-2 (lexical predication vs. sharʿī judgment) |
| DO-6 (Attribute coherence) | C-1, O-1 (reason-over-transmission inversion in attribute argument) |
| DO-13 (Philosopher's God vs. God of revelation) | T-1, T-3, O-1 (Aristotelian framework as counterfeit tribunal + later foundation mistaken for pure) |
| DO-11/12 (Trinity pressure) | K-1, C-1 (Trinitarian categories imported from a different metaphysical tradition) |
| RT-1/RT-3 (Manuscript/qirāʾāt) | K-1 (NT manuscript methods conflated with Qurʾānic transmission architecture) |
| Any case where "God must be X" appears | T-1, T-3, C-1 (usurpation pattern) |
| Any case where fiṭrī recognition is dismissed | O-2, O-3 |
| Any case where kalāmic proof is demanded | T-2, O-3 |

## Failure Test

This file has not been applied if:

- Criterion-importing, tribunal-installation, or ordering-violation elements were visible in the input, but `[Backbone Predicates] — none active` was emitted without checking the relevant predicates from the trigger mapping above.
- A backbone predicate fired (C-1, T-1, O-2, etc.) but no route consequence was recorded and no module was triggered by it.
- The compression rule was invoked to avoid checking predicates that the trigger mapping flags as minimum checks for the live NS/DO case.
