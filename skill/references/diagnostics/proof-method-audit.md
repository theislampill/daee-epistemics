# Proof-Method Audit

This file is not an argument bank. It is a taxonomy and audit surface for proof families.

The governing question is not "which proof wins?" but:

1. What proof family is being invoked?
2. What does it assume?
3. What does it actually establish?
4. Is it being used as a secondary restorative route, or as an imported grammar that claims tribunal status over all God-talk?

---

## Proof-Family Audit Table

| Family | Assumes | Typically establishes | Hidden load risk | Correct status |
|--------|---------|-----------------------|------------------|----------------|
| Fiṭrah / reminder / āyāt | recognition can be awakened once occlusion is reduced | restored attention, not discursive closure | being dismissed as "not proof" by an imported criterion | primary route in healthy cases |
| Cosmological / temporal origination | causal intelligibility; temporal origination or explanatory insufficiency of regress | uncaused source or first cause in a limited sense | collapse of successive and simultaneous series | secondary restorative route |
| Necessity / contingency | modal grammar is valid and not silently overextended | a necessary ground or non-contingent explanatory terminus | necessity/contingency treated as the only admissible proof language | secondary, not tribunal |
| Teleological / design | purposive order is explanatorily salient | intelligence or purposive source | argument from current explanatory best fit inflated into exhaustive metaphysics | secondary restorative route |
| Moral grounding | objective normativity requires adequate ontological ground | moral-source pressure toward theism | moral realism simply presupposed as neutral starting point | secondary restorative route |
| Consciousness / normative character | first-person awareness, truth-orientation, and normativity are real | pressure against reductive naturalism | gap-argument inflation or category drift | secondary restorative route |

## Necessity/Contingency Overreach

### Named route

When necessity/contingency language functions as an imported proof grammar rather than as one optional inferential tool, record:

- `proof-grammar overreach`

and route:

1. foreign-premise-detection.md if the grammar is acting as criterion or tribunal
2. philosophical-usurpation.md if it is governing what revelation may say
3. this file for the audit of what the grammar actually establishes
4. husn-al-nazar-arguments.md only if inferential deployment is still warranted afterward

### Minimal pair

- "Contingent things call for explanation." → ordinary inferential claim
- "Only necessity/contingency grammar counts as real reason about God." → overreach

### Failure test

This file has not governed if a necessity/contingency proof is answered as though its grammar were neutral and exhaustive without first auditing that claim.

---

## Route-Back Rule

Even when a proof family is appropriate, the route does not end inside the proof.

After framework-clearing and bounded inferential work:

1. return to fiṭrah, reminder, or āyāt
2. do not leave the case inside proof-comparison alone
3. do not let the proof grammar replace the restored epistemic order

---

## Failure Tests

This file has not governed the response if:

- the practitioner answers a proof challenge by dumping arguments without classifying the family first
- a proof family is credited with establishing more than it actually does
- secondary inferential routes are treated as the religion's primary epistemic foundation
- necessity/contingency language is allowed to function as neutral reason without audit
