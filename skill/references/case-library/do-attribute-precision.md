# Attribute Discourse Precision - Named Confusions and Fixtures

This file isolates the logical move going wrong in divine-attribute discourse. Most failures here occur not at the level of doctrine but at the level of the move being made.

---

## Confusion 1 - Category Mistake in Predication

**Mechanism:** A creaturely predication structure is imported into divine discourse.

**Bad fixture:** Accept the objector's predication structure and then try to soften the conclusion.

**Corrected fixture:** Identify the predication move itself as the error. The issue is not "how to patch the conclusion after accepting the creaturely structure," but "why that structure was granted authority in the first place."

**Prohibited move:** Accepting "God has attributes distinct from His essence, therefore composite" before questioning the creaturely sense of "has."

---

## Confusion 2 - Illicit Analogy

**Mechanism:** An analogy to finite personal or material realities is extended beyond its valid range.

**Corrected fixture:** Name exactly where the analogy stops carrying. Keep the valid continuity; reject the creaturely surplus.

---

## Confusion 3 - Unmarked Equivocal Predication

**Mechanism:** A term is used in a creaturely sense in the objection and then silently shifted in the reply, or vice versa.

**Corrected fixture:** Separate the predication-mode question from the first-order doctrinal or moral question.

---

## Confusion 4 - Composition Panic

**Mechanism:** Many attributes -> composition -> dependence is treated as one unbroken chain without distinguishing kinds of composition or dependence.

**Corrected fixture:** Ask which kind of composition is being asserted and which dependence claim allegedly follows. The move from logical distinction to ontological dependence must be earned, not assumed.

**Routing note:** For explicit `tarkib / iftiqar / parts / otherness` pressure, pair this confusion with M9 and `sound-reason-epistemology.md` section 6.3.

---

## Confusion 5 - Attribute-Multiplicity / Person-Multiplicity Conflation

**Mechanism:** Trinitarian person-multiplicity pressure is treated as if it were the same problem as attribute-multiplicity.

**Corrected fixture:** Keep identity problems and composition problems distinct. The Trinitarian pressure is not fixed by merely repeating the composition argument.

---

## Confusion 6 - Imported Perfection Standard as Upstream Tribunal

**Mechanism:** A philosophical perfection framework is treated as the upstream tribunal by which revelation must be judged.

**Corrected fixture:** Name the framework as one position among others, refuse its tribunal status, and only then engage the attribute question.

**Routing note:** This confusion is paired with `references/diagnostics/perfection-criterion-usurpation.md` when the imported perfection criterion is the upstream issue.

---
