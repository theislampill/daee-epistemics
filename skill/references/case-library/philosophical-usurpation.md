# Philosophical Usurpation — Named Case Family

## Definition

Philosophical usurpation is the condition in which an imported philosophical framework has been installed as the upstream authority that revelation must satisfy before its content may be accepted, discussed, or deployed. This is distinct from:

- *Holding a philosophical framework:* Everyone operates with some framework; having one is not usurpation.
- *Using philosophical arguments:* Bringing philosophical content into a theological discussion is legitimate — the tradition does this constantly.
- *Pressing a philosophical objection:* Generating a specific argument that a religious claim appears to conflict with philosophical reason is legitimate and often genuine shubhah.

Usurpation specifically occurs when the framework has been granted the chair — when it functions as the tribunal before which revelation must plead its case, and whose verdict determines what revelation may assert. At that point, the framework is not a conversation participant; it is the presiding judge.

---

## I. Telltale Features (Detection Hooks)

The following features, individually or in combination, signal philosophical usurpation. They are designed to be mechanically checkable — not taste-based judgments.

**Feature U-1: The prior constraint.** The interlocutor specifies a constraint that any acceptable account of God must satisfy *before* revealed content is introduced: "God must be simple," "God cannot act in time," "God cannot have genuine emotions," "all theological claims must satisfy the standards of analytic philosophy of religion." The constraint is drawn from the philosophical tradition and functions as a prior gate.

**Feature U-2: The required allegiance.** The interlocutor asks not "does revelation address this?" but "can revelation satisfy this [philosophical] standard?" Satisfaction of the standard is the governing question, not the content or authority of the source.

**Feature U-3: The verdict over the source.** When revealed content conflicts with the philosophical conclusion, the interlocutor treats the philosophical conclusion as decisive and the revealed content as requiring reinterpretation, allegorization, or rejection. The framework's verdict overrides the source rather than being checked against it.

**Feature U-4: The invisible assumption.** The philosophical commitment functions as background assumption — the interlocutor does not identify it as a commitment or as requiring justification. It is treated as what any serious thinker must presuppose. This is Category 4 in `reason-disambiguation.md` (inherited criterion at the reason-category level).

**Feature U-5: The asymmetric application.** The standard is applied to revelation that the framework was not designed to evaluate (e.g., Aristotelian simplicity doctrine applied to Qurʾānic descriptions of divine speech and responsiveness) while the framework's own internal tensions and assumptions are not subjected to the same scrutiny.

## II. Varieties of Philosophical Usurpation

### Type A — Aristotelian/Neo-Platonic Theism as Default Standard

**Character:** The God of perfect actuality, immutability, impassibility, and simplicity is treated as the philosophically correct conception, against which the God of revelation must be measured. The God of revelation, who speaks, commands, responds, and acts in time, appears anthropomorphic by this standard. The resolution required is allegorization or denial of the revealed attributes.

**Telltale features:** U-1, U-3, U-5. The Aristotelian framework is the constraint (U-1); when the Qurʾān or Sunnah describe divine responsiveness, the framework overrides the description (U-3); the framework's own assumptions (why is pure actuality perfection?) are not examined (U-5).

**Connected files:** DO-13 (`case-library/do-christian-extensions.md`); `prophecy-wahy-supremacy.md §I`; `do-attribute-precision.md` Confusion 6.

### Type B — Scientific Naturalism as Epistemic Gate

**Character:** The methods of natural science are treated as the complete and correct epistemology — all legitimate knowledge must pass through empirical testability or logical derivation from empirically testable premises. Revelation, the fiṭrah, tawātur fiṭrī, and non-inferential recognition are excluded from the epistemic table before the case is heard.

**Telltale features:** U-1, U-2, U-4. The standard is the prior constraint (U-1); the question asked of revelation is not "is it true?" but "can it pass scientific scrutiny?" (U-2); the standard is held as obvious and unexamined (U-4).

**Connected files:** NS-1 (`case-library/noetic-profiles.md`); V2; `foreign-premise-detection.md` tribunal type: empirical verifiability.

### Type C — Historical-Critical Methodology as Hermeneutic Authority

**Character:** The methods of secular historical criticism — source criticism, redaction analysis, form criticism, the application of naturalistic priors to transmission claims — are treated as the legitimate and neutral methodology for evaluating religious texts. The tradition's own authentication criteria (tawātur, isnād, the living recitational community) are subordinated to or displaced by the historical-critical approach.

**Telltale features:** U-2, U-3, U-5. The question is whether revelation satisfies historical-critical standards (U-2); when the tradition's transmission criteria produce a different result than historical-critical methods, the historical-critical result governs (U-3); the framework's own assumptions (methodological naturalism, suspicion of testimony) are not examined (U-5).

**Connected files:** NS-9 (`case-library/noetic-profiles.md`); V10; `case-library/revelation-transmission.md`.

### Type D — Liberal Political Philosophy as Moral Arbiter

**Character:** The principles of liberal political philosophy — individual autonomy, consent as the basis of obligation, gender equality construed in a specific way, the separation of religious and civil authority — are treated as the correct moral framework against which the tradition's ethics, jurisprudence, and social vision must be evaluated. Revealed moral prescriptions that conflict with this framework must be reinterpreted, historicized, or rejected.

**Telltale features:** U-1, U-3. The liberal principles function as prior moral constraints (U-1); when revealed prescriptions conflict, the framework's verdict governs and revelation requires reinterpretation (U-3).

**Connected files:** NS-8 (`case-library/noetic-profiles.md`); `diagnostics/seven-deformations.md` iʿtiqādāt mawrūtha forms.

## III. Route Consequences

When philosophical usurpation is confirmed, the following routing applies:

**Step 1 — Name the usurpation, not just the philosophical claim.**
Distinguish: "you hold this philosophical view" (true of everyone in some sense) from "this philosophical view is functioning as the upstream authority that revelation must satisfy." Name the second specifically. The interlocutor may not have recognized their own move.

**Step 2 — Refuse to satisfy the tribunal before examining its jurisdiction.**
The first move is not to show that revelation satisfies the usurping framework's requirements. That move ratifies the tribunal's authority. The first move is to question the framework's entitlement to the chair. Foreign-premise-detection.md Step 3 (functional role) governs: is this criterion, tribunal, prior, or definitional constraint?

**Step 3 — Apply V2 to the framework itself.**
Show the framework is one position among alternatives, is not self-grounding, and is not applied consistently. The goal is not to refute the framework (which would still leave it in a privileged position as the object being refuted) but to externalize it — to make visible that it is a contestable philosophical stance rather than neutral reason.

**Step 4 — Restore the correct authority order.**
After the framework has been loosened: articulate the tradition's authority-ordering — the sources of knowledge that the tradition recognizes, their respective standing, and why waḥy has the standing it does in the domains where philosophy is weakest. `prophecy-wahy-supremacy.md` supplies the doctrinal ground.

**Step 5 — Engage the content question only after Steps 1–4.**
Once the framework has been moved from the chair to the table — from presiding judge to one position among several — the specific philosophical claim or objection can be engaged on its own merits. At that point, DO-series content, attribute-precision fixtures, or the relevant case file may be loaded.

## IV. Prohibited Moves

**PM-1:** Satisfying the usurping framework's requirements as the primary response. This grants the tribunal's jurisdiction before examining whether it has any.

**PM-2:** Accepting "God must be X" (where X is drawn from the philosophical framework) as a premise and then defending revelation as compatible with it. This places the philosophical premise in the position of major premise and revelation in the position of minor premise that must fit it.

**PM-3:** Allegorizing or reinterpreting revealed content to fit the philosophical framework's requirements without first examining the framework's authority. This is the most common sophisticated failure mode: the defense appears intellectually serious because it engages the philosophical vocabulary, but it has already conceded the framework's jurisdiction.

**PM-4:** Attacking the philosophical framework without establishing the tradition's positive authority-ordering. Refutation of the framework alone leaves a vacuum; the positive account of why prophetic knowledge has the standing it does must accompany the critique.

---

## V. Restoration Targets

**Primary:** Relocate the philosophical framework from the tribunal position to the position of one contestable philosophical stance among several — a position that must justify its own assumptions before it may function as a constraint on what revelation may assert.

**Secondary:** Restore the correct epistemic-authority ordering: waḥy on established transmission grounds carries epistemic authority in the domains where it speaks; philosophical argument is a clarifying and remediating tool, not a gate.

**Tertiary:** For the content question: once the framework has been moved from the chair, engage the specific philosophical objection or attribute claim using the matched content module (DO-13, do-attribute-precision.md, prophecy-wahy-supremacy.md as appropriate).
