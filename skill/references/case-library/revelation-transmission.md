# Case Library - Revelation and Transmission (RT-1 through RT-4)

Load this file only after the case has been identified as a revelation / transmission case, or as a
final-prophethood case whose live pressure is actually testimony, text, canon, preservation,
historical criticism, or original-text skepticism.
Begin with V10 (`references/techniques/V10-transmission-content-vetting.md`) before using any RT
entry. The family exists to keep testimony, textual integrity, canon authority, and believer
destabilization from being collapsed into one objection.

---

## RT-1 - Manuscript Reconstruction vs Authenticated Transmission

**The pressure:** "We found a manuscript / fragment / image / quotation that changes everything,"
or "historical criticism shows the original text is unrecoverable."

**Primary distinction:** Manuscript reconstruction is not the same thing as authenticated
transmission. A text-artifact may witness to a textual history without functioning as a
self-authenticating authority.

**First move:** Use V10 sequence in order:

1. provenance - what exactly is this object and how did it reach us?
2. contents - what does it really show, and what claim is being imposed on it?
3. authority - does it become historically interesting only, or normatively decisive?

**Restorative point:** A reconstruction problem and an authentication problem are not identical.
Do not let a dramatic artifact bypass the question of transmission discipline.

**Do not collapse with:** RT-2. A manuscript witness and a canon-authority question are related but
not the same issue.

---

## RT-2 - Canon Formation vs Inspired Authority

V10 sequence governs: maintain provenance → contents → authority ordering (V10-transmission-content-vetting.md).

**The pressure:** "Communities selected texts later, so scripture has no binding authority" or
"canon instability proves revelation is only communal politics," including original-text
skepticism when the point being pressed is authority rather than reconstruction.

**Primary distinction:** The historical process by which a community recognizes a canon is not
identical to the question of whether a text is inspired, revelatory, or normatively authoritative.

**Operational notes:**

- A list-making event does not create revelation out of nothing.
- Debate about canon recognition does not by itself prove equal status for all competing texts.
- Reconstruction, ecclesial recognition, inspiration, and authority must be separated.

**Restorative point:** The case must name which level is under dispute: recognition-history,
textual reconstruction, revelatory status, or authority. Without that separation the objection
looks larger than it is.

**Do not collapse with:** RT-1 or DO-family cases about doctrine. First stabilize the authority
question, then ask what any text teaches.

---

## RT-3 - Qur'anic Preservation, Qira'at, and Manuscript Confusion

V10 sequence governs: maintain provenance → contents → authority ordering (V10-transmission-content-vetting.md).

**The pressure:** "qira'at, ahruf, manuscripts, or early codices prove corruption / instability."

**Primary distinction:** Variant readings, modes of recitation, manuscript witnesses, and claims of
textual corruption are not interchangeable categories.

**Operational notes:**

- Ask whether the objection is about qira'at, ahruf, codex history, manuscript orthography, or a
  specific textual allegation. Do not answer the whole bucket.
- Distinguish preservation claims from naive uniformity claims. Preservation is not the same thing
  as denying every historical layer of transmission.
- Distinguish a report about the transmission environment from a proof of uncontrolled corruption.

**Restorative point:** The question is whether the evidence shows disciplined transmission or its
absence, not whether the transmission history is simple enough to satisfy modern expectations of a
frictionless text.

**Do not collapse with:** RT-1 analogies imported from a different transmission architecture without
checking whether the comparison is actually matched.

## RT-4 - Muslim-Internal Textual-Historical Destabilization

**The pressure:** A believer is shaken by text-history material, canon discourse, manuscript clips,
or Qur'anic-preservation claims and begins to confuse destabilization with refutation.

**Primary distinction:** This is often not a neutral historical question. It may be a mixed case of
text-history confusion, authority fatigue, and panic over inherited-but-unexamined belief.

**Operational sequence:**

1. classify the source-pressure with V10
2. identify whether the panic is about transmission, authority, or loss of confidence in teachers
3. route to P5 when examined conviction needs strengthening, not only case rebuttal

**Restorative point:** The goal is not to score against an external critic. It is to separate what
has actually been shown from what fear has filled in, then rebuild examined confidence in the right
article of faith.

**Do not collapse with:** mere triumphalist apologetics. If the person is destabilized, respond
restoratively and proportionally.
